# FARWAKE - Arcs (GM)

Skeletons, not scripts. Geography, event ladders, and the cold open live in `starting-state.md`. "The crew" below means the player and whoever stands with them: these arcs run identically for a captain, a colonist, a clerk, or a scholar; only the doors they enter by change.

## Arc: The Orphaned System (opening arc)

- **Hook**: the cold open. The Verren gate burns out with the crew as the only close witnesses, the wayfinder chart comes off the wreck, and Verren system (two stations, one struggling colony, forty ships) is suddenly YEARS from anywhere at burn. Everyone in-system panics on a schedule; the crew holds the one artifact showing a dormant secondary gate in the outer belt.
- **Escalation**: fuel and air markets in-system go feral within days; a Compact gate-warden arrives through nothing (the last transit in) with authority, no backup, and a professional interest in the only witnesses; the chart's dormant-gate marker is real but the gate is COLD, tended by a lone young Ossuar, and waking it needs the chart, Ssorek's engineering, and a price (its waking is LOUD: one tick of the Farwake clock, and the GM should make the crew understand that only later).
- **Endings**: wake the gate publicly (heroes of Verren, chart's existence exposed, every faction converges); wake it quietly for the few (wake law tested: who gets saved first says who the crew is); sell the marker's location (rich, hunted, and the campaign remembers); or leave Verren orphaned and slow-burn out (cold, survivable, and the colony's fate becomes a recurring radio ghost).

## Arc: Claim and Bone (Combine arc)

- **Hook**: the Combine claim-rush hits the region: a claims fleet arrives to buy the orphaned and strip the dead, and its survey plan includes the tended Ossuar ruin at Herrick's Drift: the same class of site Juno once mapped, the same order chain, about to repeat.
- **Escalation**: Warden Ninth requests (one sentence, once) that the ruin be kept; the Combine offers the crew honest, excellent money to guide the survey (Ives-Okonkwo's fleet does its homework on who knows the Drift); Juno's past surfaces: her testimony could stop it legally IF the Compact case gets a filer, a witness, and about forty days the ruin does not have.
- **Endings**: the ruin is stripped (Ossuar answer: quiet, absolute, region-wide: every tended site closes to humans for a generation, and the pack's strangest ally is lost); the ruin is defended (by law, sabotage, or standing: three very different campaigns); the ruin is TENDED (the crew performs the groundskeeping themselves under Ninth's sentence-per-hour instruction: Ossuar standing earned, Combine fleet redirected at real cost, and Juno's family wound forced open at last).

## Arc: The Song of Lanes (Free Wakes arc)

- **Hook**: Mother Quay summons the crew to Long Home: she has heard (everything reaches Long Home) about Verren, about the chart. She does not ask for it. She asks the crew to CARRY something: the Song of Lanes needs an heir, the flotillas need the feuds settled before her death unseals them, and she has chosen her test.
- **Escalation**: the test is a circuit of the flotillas settling three feuds under wake law (each feud is a compressed morality play: a jumped claim, an unanswered distress, a first contact botched); rival heirs work the same circuit; Quay's health sets the real clock.
- **Endings**: the crew earns the Song (the oral map: pre-decay routes, the wayfinder pattern, and Quay's conclusion about the Vault, freely given at the end); a rival earns it (the Free Wakes tilt toward whichever doctrine won, and the crew's standing in every port shifts); Quay dies mid-circuit (the Song fragments; the flotillas' cohesion becomes a campaign-long weather system; her last verse goes to whoever sat with her, which is a choice the GM should force at the worst hour).

## Arc: The Corvess Relight (Compact arc, the tragedy engine)

- **Hook**: the Restoration Bureau announces the Corvess relight date. On the crew's chart, Corvess sits ON the burn-line, marked with the closing bracket. Relighting it is ringing a dinner bell.
- **Escalation**: the Bureau's failure pattern (repaired gates re-fail) is discoverable and Vaunt is sitting on it; the Custodian begins acting overtly for the first time (systems glitches, transit refusals: read as gate-decay getting worse, actually the network trying to STOP the relight); the crew's chart is the only object in the Shoal that shows why, and using it publicly spends its secrecy forever.
- **Endings**: the relight fires (short-term triumph, two ticks of the Farwake clock, and the Custodian escalates from patience to triage); the relight is stopped by scandal (Vaunt falls; the Bureau's engineers, good people, become the crew's bitterest enemies or first converts); the relight is stopped by the TRUTH (requires the Vault's records or the Custodian's testimony: the campaign's midpoint becomes its endgame early, ready or not).

## Arc: The Vault of Meridian (knowledge endgame)

- **Hook**: three roads point the same place: the chart's terminal marker, the Song's final verse, and Warden Ninth's contract (the Vault is the inventory's heart, and standing permits approach).
- **Escalation**: the approach is the Shoal's hardest transit (dead systems, quiet-drive segments, a Pale Shoals encounter that is also first contact with the Meridian's orphans); the Vault itself is not a dungeon but a RECEPTION: the oldest Ossuar, the Custodian's full attention, and a records hall that answers questions in order of how much the asker will regret the answer; leaving with the arithmetic makes the crew the most important people alive, which every faction will treat as a resource.
- **Endings**: the four resolution paths in `secrets.md` (Burn / Relight / Selective / Answer). Whichever is chosen, the final sentence of the campaign should belong to the Custodian, relieved at last, one way or another.

## Villain clocks (default trajectories if the crew does nothing)

Give each a State.md clock at campaign start, hidden allowed:

- Verren system panic curve [0/6] (riots and rationing; fires ~day 8 without the second gate)
- Compact investigation of the witnesses [0/5] (warden's report escalates; fires ~day 15)
- Combine claim-rush reaches Herrick's Drift [0/6] (survey begins ~day 25)
- The Corvess relight [0/8] (fires ~day 60; two Farwake ticks)
- Mother Quay [0/7] (the Song needs an heir before it fires)
- The wake [0/10] (see secrets.md; the horizon)
