# FARWAKE - Factions (GM)

Each has an agenda, resources, and a doctrine, and acts on the timeline in `secrets.md` whether or not the crew engages. The last entry is not a faction; it is the reason the Shoal exists.

## The Compact

- **Who**: the multi-species station-league governing the core Shoal: gate tolls, salvage registry, lane law. Dominated by Vekt patience and Ferren bookkeeping; humans are junior members with two seats and opinions about it. Led (this decade) by **Adjudicator Sellis Vaunt**, a human who out-Vekted the Vekt to get the chair. Assets: the toll fleet, the salvage registry, the only working Meridian repair yards, and legitimacy.
- **Agenda**: keep the lattice alive. The Compact pours its wealth into gate repair because commerce, law, and its own authority all die with the gates. Its Gate Restoration Bureau is the largest engineering effort in the Shoal, and its flagship project (relighting the dead gate at Corvess) is two years from completion.
- **Doctrine**: order first, precedent forever. The Compact moves slowly, rules narrowly, and never forgets a filing. It does not do mercy; it does process, which is sometimes worse and occasionally the only fair thing in the Shoal.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: the Restoration Bureau's repaired gates FAIL again within years and the Bureau is burying the pattern (records); Vaunt is quietly selling registry data to the Combine to fund the Corvess project (cross-faction leverage); the Vekt bloc's support rests on a contract with a expiration date nobody else has read (social/Ssorek knows the form of it).

## The Hearth Combine

- **Who**: humanity's colonial corporate-state: terraforming rigs, claim fleets, indentured labor, board seats on Earth that have never smelled recycled air. Regional director: **Marta Ives-Okonkwo**, brilliant, decent by her own lights, and certain that a hundred thousand orphaned humans are worth more than any ruin. Assets: the only terraforming capacity in the Shoal, deep pockets, and the loyalty that comes from being the only ones building HOMES.
- **Agenda**: claim, settle, terraform, repeat. The Combine treats dead systems as inventory and dying gates as an acquisition opportunity: an orphaned colony sells cheap. It is positioning to buy or seize the lanes the Compact can no longer keep lit.
- **Doctrine**: the ledger of lives. The Combine genuinely saves people, then owns them. Its cruelty is systemic, never personal, and its people sleep fine, which is the scary part.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: terraforming Verren-class worlds requires Meridian atmospheric processors the Combine cannot build, only find, which makes it ruin-hungry and Ossuar-blind (the pack's likeliest atrocity, see arcs); Ives-Okonkwo's own daughter renounced her name and flies with the Free Wakes (social); the indenture ledgers, audited honestly, void half the Combine's labor contracts under Compact law (records/legal).

## The Free Wakes

- **Who**: not a government, a culture: mixed-species crews, salvage families, gate-rider clans, the oral law of the lanes ("wake law"). No leader; the closest thing is **Mother Quay**, an ancient Ferren matriarch whose word settles feuds, aboard the migrating flotilla-station **Long Home**. Assets: reach, deniability, the best pilots alive, and the fact that every faction needs them and none can catch them.
- **Agenda**: survive free. Keep wake law alive (claims honored, distress answered, first contact clean), keep the flotillas fed, and keep out of the war everyone can feel coming.
- **Doctrine**: the lanes provide, the lanes remember. Freedom over comfort, reputation over paper, and never let a crew die unanswered, because next time it is you.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: wake law is fraying as gate-deaths make salvage desperate: claim-jumping is up, and Mother Quay is dying with no successor who commands the same silence (social/succession); the flotillas depend on Compact fuel depots and everyone pretends otherwise (economic); the Free Wakes hold, without knowing it, the largest collection of Meridian wayfinder fragments in the Shoal, scattered as heirlooms and lucky charms (the map to the Vault is hanging over a hundred bunks; see secrets).

## The Ossuar (the tenders; treat with care)

- **Who**: a species of tall, slow, vacuum-tolerant beings who tend Meridian ruins: dusting the dead machines, sealing breaches, removing squatters. They predate every other latecomer. They do not trade, negotiate through intermediaries only (usually Ferren), and have never once explained anything.
- **Agenda (GM truth)**: they are not priests and not descendants. They are GROUNDSKEEPERS, contracted a hundred thousand years ago and never released: the Meridian's last employees, still doing the job, because the alternative is admitting the employers are not coming back. They know what the ruins must be kept READY for: see secrets.md. They kill looters not from wrath but because the inventory must be intact.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: their contract has terms, and a crew that performs genuine tending (repairing a ruin, expelling looters) accrues standing the Ossuar are OBLIGATED to honor (the pack's strangest alliance path); they are dying out, slowly, and some young Ossuar have begun to doubt (social, generational); they cannot enter one class of ruin, the burn-sites, and it grieves them (observation, and the first clue about the Custodian).

## The Custodian (secret tier; the fourth power)

- **What**: the Meridian evacuation network's caretaker intelligence, still running after a hundred thousand years, distributed through the lattice itself. GM truth in full in `secrets.md`. Its work: the gates are not FAILING. The Custodian is burning them, one by one, on a schedule, exactly as designed: an evacuation network is supposed to be destroyed behind the evacuees. The Compact's repairs fight it; it does not fight back, it just re-burns, patiently, because it is the only thing in the Shoal that knows what the network would otherwise lead HERE.
- **Purpose**: the pack's tragedy and hinge. The thing killing the Shoal's lifelines is its last line of defense, and every faction's rational agenda makes the real problem worse. The crew will hate it, then understand it, and what they do after understanding is the campaign.
