# FARWAKE - Secrets (GM)

The truths behind the campaign. Nothing here reaches the player except through play.

## What the Meridian ran from

The Meridian did not fall. They EVACUATED, in good order, a hundred thousand years ago, ahead of the thing their own expansion had attracted: the **Silence**. Not a fleet and not a species in any social sense: a slow interstellar ecology, a swarm-organism that homes on the electromagnetic and gate-resonance noise of industrial civilizations and grazes them down to geology. It does not hate. It migrates. It is why the galaxy is quiet.

The lattice was their evacuation network: build corridors, move everyone OUT (where, even the Custodian does not know; the destination logs left with the last ship), then burn the network behind you so the corridors cannot be followed. The Shoal is not a civilization's home. It is a fire escape, and everyone now living in it has moved into the stairwell.

## The burn, and why gates die

The Custodian (see factions.md) has been executing the burn for a hundred thousand years, slowed catastrophically by damage and by latecomers rebuilding what it destroys. Gate "decay" is the burn: deliberate, scheduled, merciful in intent. The Compact's Restoration Bureau is, with total sincerity and excellent engineering, dismantling the Shoal's only defense.

**Why Sol's gate opened**: a burn-order failure. Sol's node was queued for destruction; a Custodian fault (damage from an Ossuar-tended ruin fire, ~40 years ago) flipped the order to WAKE. The Custodian has been trying to correct it since. Humanity exists in the Shoal because of a typo in an apocalypse, and the Sol Gate is on the burn schedule. That fact, discovered, changes every human faction's mathematics overnight.

## The Farwake signal (the campaign's deep timer)

The Silence found the lattice's edge. Far out past the Shoal's rim, gates have been waking briefly and screaming, then going dark: the Custodian burning nodes AHEAD of something moving along the old corridors. Plotted on a chart, the burn-line points inward, toward the Shoal, a system every few years and accelerating.

Track as a hidden campaign clock: **[the wake 0/10]**, one tick per ~15 in-game days or per major provocation (a gate RELIT along the burn-line, large-scale gate-resonance noise, the Corvess relight firing). At full: the Silence's leading edge reaches the rim systems, and the campaign's map starts losing places. Like HOLLOWCROWN's Emberheart, this clock is meant to LOOM; play should resolve the campaign before it resolves the campaign. The Vault (below) holds the honest arithmetic.

## The wayfinder chart (the opening's inheritance)

Meridian wayfinders are personal navigation shards; most are dead trinkets. The one recovered in the cold open is a LIVE command-grade chart: it shows the lattice as the Custodian sees it: gates alive, dead, queued for burn (marked with a closing bracket every spacer will learn to dread), and the burn-line of the Farwake signal. It also shows one marker nothing else shows: the **Vault of Meridian**, the network's terminal depot, at the Shoal's gravitational heart. Every faction that learns what the chart shows will want it at any price; the Custodian, for its part, registers the chart's carrier as SOMETHING IT CAN FINALLY TALK TO (see arcs: Doorkeeper parallel deliberately inverted: this time the office is real and the employer is a machine).

## The Vault of Meridian (knowledge endgame)

The last depot: sealed, tended by the oldest Ossuar, holding the evacuation's final records. Inside: what the Silence is, the full burn schedule, the Sol Gate's queue position, the honest arithmetic of the Farwake clock, and the Meridian's parting gift: schematics for the **quiet drive**, slow interstellar travel that makes no noise the Silence can hear: survival without gates, at the price of generations. Whoever holds the Vault's contents chooses what the Shoal gets to know and what it gets to become.

## Resolution paths (seed all, force none)

- **Complete the burn**: side with the Custodian: let the lattice die on schedule, Sol included. Every system becomes an island; the quiet-drive schematics make it survivable, barely, over generations. Safety as amputation.
- **Relight the lattice**: the Compact's dream fully realized: prosperity, reunion, orphaned colonies saved, and a lighthouse lit for the Silence. The Vault's arithmetic says exactly how long the party lasts. Some crews will choose it anyway, and the pack does not judge them for it.
- **The selective network**: the brokered path: a minimal lattice, burn the rest, quiet-drive the gaps, EM discipline as law: civilization on tiptoe. Requires leverage over every faction, Ossuar standing, and the Custodian's cooperation, which requires someone it trusts holding the chart. The hardest path; the one the pack is proudest of.
- **Answer the Silence**: the unknowable option: use the chart and the Vault's records to go OUT, along the burn-line, and look at it. The Meridian never tried talking. The pack defines what is found only this far: it is not a conversation, it is an ecology, but ecologies can be redirected, fed, or starved, and one crew with perfect information is the first entity in a hundred millennia positioned to try. GM builds the rest from play; permadeath rules very much in force.

## First-contact truths (the discovery economy's deep layer)

- **The Chorus**: the derelict-whisperers are real: colonial fungal intelligences that settle dead ships and RUN them, slowly learning each hull like a language. They are not haunting; they are homesteading, and they experience crews as weather. One colony (Whisper-of-Hulls, npcs.md) has done something unprecedented: chosen personhood. What the Chorus network concludes from that experiment depends on how Whisper is treated.
- **The Ferren**: the Shoal's oldest surviving latecomers (nine hundred years). Their oral lane-maps encode pre-decay lattice routes; Mother Quay herself is the last who holds the full Song of Lanes, and she is dying. A crew that earns the Song holds a map worth more than the Combine.
- **The Pale Shoals** (rumor tier): void-adapted post-biologicals glimpsed at the rim: GM truth: Meridian stragglers, descendants of the evacuation's left-behind, changed beyond recognition and TERRIFIED of the relit gates. First contact with them is first contact with the Meridian's orphans, and they remember the burn drill.
- **New species at the edges**: the Shoal is vast and the pack seeds only its centerpieces. Per GAME.md discovery economics: crews that survey, listen, and ask genuinely CAN find species and ruins no faction knows: invent on your own judgment, promote to canon, and make first-contact conduct matter every time. Not every world holds a wonder; the ones that do remember how you arrived.

## Hard rule the crew learns the hard way

Gate transit resonance: every gate transit is LOUD in the spectrum the Silence grazes on. The Farwake clock's provocation list exists because of this; a crew that learns it gains the Shoal's most unwelcome superpower: the ability to compute, roughly, what any given journey costs the future. Never state the mechanic; let the Vault's arithmetic, the Custodian's behavior, and one Ossuar sentence ("you were heard") teach it.
