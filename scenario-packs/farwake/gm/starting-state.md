# FARWAKE - Starting State (GM)

## Opening: Day 0, the Verren system, the hour the gate dies

Verren is a nothing system: one gate, two stations, a stubborn dome colony, forty-odd ships. Today it becomes the most important nothing in the Shoal.

**The inciting event is system-wide, so the cold open stages WHEREVER creation puts the player**: a salvage berth in the belt, a hydroponics shift in Meridian Down, a customs desk on High Verren, a bar, a survey camp, a cell. The gate dies for everyone at once:

1. **The gate dies screaming.** Verren's gate flares across every band: not failure noise, a STRUCTURED transmission (the Custodian's burn-order, though no one knows it), then nothing, forever. Verren is now years from anywhere at burn. Light-lag means different places learn at different moments; stage the player's location hearing it in its own way (instruments, sirens, a comms tech going white, the market simply stopping). Let the comms fill up in real time.
2. **The inheritance finds the player within a day**, shaped to where they are. The wreck-claim in the belt (a hauler dark nine years) holds a live Meridian command chart and a Chorus colony that has spent nine years learning what the dying crew's songs meant. Route whichever thread fits the player's life: a spacer is aboard or near the claim when the burn-flare wakes both; a colonist or stationer gets the dying salvage captain's sealed effects, a debt called in, or Whisper-of-Hulls walking out of a cargo transfer asking, carefully, for asylum; an official gets the warden's investigation (below) landing on their desk with the chart as evidence nobody else recognizes. However it routes, by nightfall the player holds the chart (see secrets.md) or knows exactly who does.
3. **The law is stranded too.** One transit made it in before the burn: Compact gate-warden **Halli Dren**, corvette, jurisdiction over a gate that no longer exists, no backup, no way home. She attaches herself to the event's best witnesses and evidence, which within a day means the player. Professional, exhausted, on the record.

First menu should offer (roughly, shaped to the player's seat): cooperate with Dren straight, hide what you hold, move toward the colony or station as the panic math starts, interrogate the chart or Whisper first, or something else. Every option is real; in this system, from today, every resource spent is gone.

## Character creation (interview, in-fiction framing)

Three questions, asked in the quiet before the flare reaches them:

1. **Who are you in the Shoal, and what carried you (or your family) through a gate?** (trade and origin seed 2-3 starting traits and one tool; every kind of life qualifies: spacer, farmer, clerk, medic, scholar, drifter)
2. **What do you call home: a hull, a dome, a station ring, or nothing yet?** (seeds the home base and its people; a HULL makes the ship real from scene one; anything else makes a ship of your own an ambition the campaign will honor)
3. **What promise are you moving toward?** (a debt, a person, a place, a repair: seeds the private clock and quietly tells the GM what this campaign is about for this player)

Aptitudes: 4/3/3/2/2 from the answers (the trade places the 4). Reflect as fiction, never numbers.

**The default start (use it when the player has no strong pull).** The open seat serves players who arrive knowing who they want to be; a player who shrugs at question 1 should not be handed a blank galaxy. For them, recommend the pack's sharpest opening without asking twice: home is a hull, the salvage cutter, docked to the claim when the gate dies: chart, Whisper, and Dren all land in the first hour, and every other life in the Shoal remains reachable from that deck. Freedom is the pack's shape; the cutter is its handle.

**The ship (if home is a hull, or once one is earned)**: default a Free Wake salvage cutter, player names it (registry default: LONG ODDS). Honest, tired, paid for or nearly. Ship state opens in State.md: reaction mass 62%, scrubber margin 40 days crew-of-four, hull patches 6, spares thin, gate-toll credit nearly nil. Creation answers become her quirks (a hatch that needs kicking, a drive harmonic her engineer calls her voice). Any ship in play is a character: write her a dossier. **Shipless starts**: passage is bought, worked, or owed; the crew seeds in npcs.md are met in the opening days regardless, each a doorway onto a deck.

Starting inventory (personal): vac-suit or dome-wear per origin, one trade tool, one sidearm OR one specialist kit (not both), scrip that won't survive a month, and one meaningful personal object (player defines; it will matter).

## The Verren system and near lanes (gazetteer, GM reference)

- **Verren**: the orphaned stage. **Meridian Down** (dome colony, 3,100 souls, twenty days of calm before rationing math goes public); **High Verren** (transfer station, half the system's fuel); **the Belt claim-fields** (the crew's ground, a dozen rival salvage crews, wake law under strain); **the cold gate** (dormant, outer belt, one young Ossuar tending it: the opening arc's prize).
- **Herrick's Drift** (one dead-lane transit out): tended Ossuar ruin complex on a tidally locked world: ground exploration showcase: vacuum galleries, intact Meridian machinery, and the Combine survey inbound (arcs).
- **Long Home** (mobile): the Free Wake flotilla-station; markets, feuds, Mother Quay, and every rumor in the Shoal three weeks late and mostly true.
- **Corvess** (via core lanes): the Bureau's relight project: scaffolded dead gate, three thousand engineers, the tragedy engine.
- **The rim, the burn-line, the Vault**: see secrets.md; reached only when the campaign reaches for them.

## Ground and space event ladders (per transit / per landfall)

- **Space**: contact bogeys (mostly honest ships with dishonest fuel margins), claim disputes, distress calls (wake law: answering is identity, not charity), Compact inspections, gate-resonance ghosts near dead gates, and (rare, escalating) Custodian anomalies.
- **Ground**: every world gets its own physics, written at first landfall (gravity, air, weather, what listens). Ladder: environmental turns (suit margins are clocks), ruins (tended vs untended: VERY different ladders), wildlife (invent per world; not every world is empty and not every empty world stays that way), survey rivals, and first-contact candidates per secrets.md discovery economics.

## Board state at Day 0

- The chart: live, aboard, and not yet understood; its dormant-gate marker is the only mercy in the system
- Whisper-of-Hulls: aboard, polite, unprecedented; the Chorus network does not know yet
- Warden Dren: docking within hours; stranded, dutiful, incorruptible by money (leverage is another matter)
- Verren panic curve [0/6]: ticks with every public revelation; the colony's rationing math goes public on day 8
- The Combine: claim-rush fleet eleven days out from the region, Herrick's Drift on its survey plan
- Mother Quay: has already heard the gate died; expects the crew at Long Home inside a month, though no invitation has been sent
- The wake [0/10]: far away, patient, and pointed here; the Verren burn was the Custodian staying ahead of it
- Weather (system-wide): the belt's long calm; Verren's sun is quiet; every storm in this opening is human
