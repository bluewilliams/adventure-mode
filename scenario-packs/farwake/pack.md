# FARWAKE

A dark space-epic pack for Adventure Mode. Thirty dying star systems, stations and dome colonies, ships and the people who fix them, faction politics one bad season from war, and a region full of species who all arrived too late. Start as anyone; the Shoal is big enough to spend a life in.

## Player-safe brief (what every spacer in the Shoal knows)

Thirty-eight years ago the Sol Gate woke on its own and humanity walked through an open door into someone else's ruin. The Shoal: some thirty star systems strung on a lattice of gates built by the Meridian, a civilization that has been gone a hundred thousand years. Nobody knows where they went. Everybody knows they left in a hurry.

Humanity is the newest arrival, not the first. The Shoal is full of latecomers like us, scavenging the same dead infrastructure. Every spacer knows:

- **The gates are dying.** A gate that hums today can go dark in your lifetime, and a dark gate means a system is suddenly years away at burn instead of hours. Whole colonies have been orphaned. Once in a rare while, a dead gate wakes instead, and nobody can say why either happens.
- **Three banners fly in the lanes.** The Compact runs the core systems: multi-species station-law, gate tolls, order with a very long waiting list to matter. The Hearth Combine is humanity's corporate colonial engine, hungry for claims and unbothered by who was there first. The Free Wakes are the lanes themselves: salvagers, gate-riders, mixed crews with no flag but the oral law of the lanes. You are probably one of them.
- **The neighbors are real.** The Vekt trade hard and keep contracts with terrifying literalism. The Ferren are small, everywhere, underestimated, and remember more than anyone. Ossuar tend the Meridian ruins and do not explain themselves; crews that loot tended ruins stop being heard from. And spacers swear some derelicts are inhabited by something that whispers through the hull plating. Spacers swear a lot of things.
- **The Meridian left everything and took nothing.** Their ruins are intact, their machines patient, their tools priceless, and their absence the loudest fact in the Shoal. Nobody serious asks IF something made them run. The serious question is where it is now.
- **Salvage law is real law.** A registered claim is life and death; a jumped claim starts feuds that outlive ships. The lanes remember a ship's name longer than its hull lasts.
- **Every kind of life gets lived here.** Dome farmers, station medics, Compact clerks, Combine agents, ruin scholars, flotilla kids who have never felt gravity. The lanes are the Shoal's arteries, but most people are not spacers, and every one of them felt it when the gates started dying.

Character creation starts with three questions the GM will ask: who are you in the Shoal and what carried you (or your family) through a gate; what do you call home; and what promise are you moving toward.
