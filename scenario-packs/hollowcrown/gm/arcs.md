# HOLLOWCROWN - Arcs (GM)

Skeletons, not scripts: hooks, escalation beats, multiple endings. The Deeproads geography and event ladders live in `starting-state.md`.

## Arc: The Carter's Compass (opening arc)

- **Hook**: the cold open. A dying carter from the changed caravan presses a dwarven lodestone compass into the player's hand; its needle points DOWN, and slightly sideways, toward the nearest failing seal. Three parties want it within a day: the Company (evidence to bury), the Vigil (relic to impound), Brakka (she can READ it; it is a seal-warden's tool that shows door integrity).
- **Escalation**: the compass reveals doors failing in sequence, accelerating; following its bearings finds the next door BEFORE it opens (unbearable tactical value); the Court Below notices someone holding a warden's tool and assumes an office that comes with obligations: a herald addresses the player as "Doorkeeper" in front of everyone.
- **Endings**: surrender it to a faction (each reshapes the mid-game differently); keep it and grow into the Doorkeeper title (main-path spine); give it BACK below (first peaceful contact, unlocks the Sunken Ring parley early).

## Arc: The Missing Foothold (Reclaimant arc)

- **Hook**: Ulfra's son Dain led twelve Reclaimants to hold a Middle Dark waystation; nine days silent. Ulfra hires, begs, or leverages the player (Brakka goes regardless).
- **Escalation**: the waystation is intact, provisioned, and empty except for twelve bedrolls and one tunnel-singer's drum, still faintly humming; tracks lead DOWN, unforced; found alive at a seal-shrine near the Sunken Ring: they answered a summons they heard in their own dreams and are half-convinced going home means going ALL the way home. Dain is furthest gone: stone-freckled, calm, fluent in Old Vholic, arguing his people's ancestral case to a Hollowed lawspeaker, and WINNING points.
- **Endings**: drag them back (saves bodies, breaks hearts, Ulfra owes you and hates owing); let them choose (some stay; the first living-Hollowed household in three centuries becomes a diplomatic beachhead for the Third Oath); Halden learns of it first and arrives with fire (the pack's ugliest battle; every faction re-aligns around what the player did in it).

## Arc: Sample 7 (the Shard arc, main spine)

- **Hook**: converging evidence: Cass Werrin's drunk arithmetic, Fenn's nightmares, the compass bearings all tilting toward Lanternwatch itself, a herald's recitation naming "the seventh taking."
- **Escalation**: confirming the Shard sits in Sarn's vault (heist, audit, or bargain: three genres, player's pick); holding it (it is warm, it is QUIET in a way that eats other sound, and every magical art near it works free: the temptation is the trap); the Court's escalation ladder shifts from asking to taking, with dates (see timeline).
- **Endings**: the four resolution paths in `secrets.md` (Return / Destroy / Wield / Third Oath). The Gravemouth descent for Return is the pack's endgame dungeon crawl: every tier, every earned ally, every unpaid debt shows up on the way down. The carrier of the Shard marks fastest; choosing WHO carries it is the arc's real climax, and the best answer might not be the player.

## Arc: The Last Door (Vigil arc)

- **Hook**: Kessilde's seizure of the Great Stair has a date. Maren learns it and cannot square it with her oath; she brings it to the player because there is no one else outside the chain of command.
- **Escalation**: the Company learns something is coming and hires every blade in the province (including, pointedly, the player); Kessilde offers the player a private audience and the closest thing to the truth anyone on the surface has ("I would rather kill this city than watch it be digested"); the seizure itself: lantern-knights on the gatehouse, Chainmen on the walls, and a herald choosing that exact hour to walk up the Stair reciting.
- **Endings**: the seizure succeeds (Lanternwatch starves toward winter; smuggling through the Drowned Door becomes the economy; the Vigil holds a door it cannot afford); it fails (Halden's faction blames Kessilde's softness; the order fractures; the doors stand undefended); the player brokers a third thing (joint wardenship, the first brick of the Third Oath); or the herald's arrival mid-battle turns everything sideways (nothing unites a battlefield like a walking dead kingdom asking for its property back, politely, in two languages).

## Arc: The Archive Under Glass (knowledge endgame)

- **Hook**: every deep faction rumor eventually points at the sealed Lawspeakers' Archive in the Sunken Ring; Brakka can sing the dome open; the Court permits approach ONLY under peace-sign or stealth that actually holds.
- **Escalation**: the descent itself (the Sunken Ring is the Hollowed capital: not a gauntlet, a CITY, and manners matter more than steel); the testament's contents (secrets.md) land the campaign's full truth including the Emberheart arithmetic; leaving with it is a choice about every faction's future.
- **Endings**: truth published (the province reorganizes around reality; every leader's reaction is in their file); truth traded (to one faction: pick the world you are building); truth sealed again (the player joins three centuries of people who decided others were not ready, and the campaign judges no one, but the epilogue remembers).

## Villain clocks (default trajectories if the player does nothing)

Give each a State.md clock at campaign start, hidden allowed:

- Vigil seizure of the Great Stair [0/5] (fires ~day 20)
- Court escalation: ask -> search -> take [0/6] (herald ~day 12, searches ~day 30, taking ~day 45)
- Company counter-army [0/4] (arms race with the Vigil; fires within days of the seizure clock)
- Emberheart quickening [0/9] (see secrets.md; the campaign's horizon)
