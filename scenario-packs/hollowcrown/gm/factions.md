# HOLLOWCROWN - Factions (GM)

Each faction has an agenda, resources, and a doctrine, and acts on the timeline in `secrets.md` whether or not the player engages. The fourth "faction" is below, and it is the reason the other three exist.

## The Ledger Company

- **Who**: a chartered mercantile company holding the crown's monopoly on Deeproad trade; ~200 clerks, gate-wardens, and contracted blades in Lanternwatch, led by **Factor Imra Sarn**, a self-made shipping heiress who signed the deep-survey charter six years ago. Assets: the Great Stair gatehouse, the toll ledgers, a private delver corps ("the Chainmen"), and more debt paper on the province than the crown itself holds.
- **Agenda**: keep the doors open and the tolls flowing. Every open door is a new trade route; every scare that closes a gate costs thousands. Sarn buys silence about incidents, underwrites delves she should not, and is quietly mapping routes the Vigil has not found.
- **Doctrine**: everything is a contract. The Company keeps written terms to the letter and betrays every spirit. It does not murder; it forecloses, blacklists, and indentures. Its violence arrives holding paperwork.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: the toll ledgers are double-booked and an honest audit would void the crown charter (infiltration/clerical); Sarn's own deep-survey report from six years ago is the thing she most needs buried, and her surveyor is still alive to testify (see Cass Werrin, npcs.md); the Chainmen are paid in debt-forgiveness, not coin, and a person who buys out a Chainman's paper owns their loyalty (social/economic).

## The Vigil of the Last Door

- **Who**: a militant religious order older than the province, sworn "to keep what was closed, closed." ~80 lantern-knights and twice that in lay brothers, motherhouse at Aldermoor, led by **Warden-Abbess Kessilde**, 70, iron-spined, losing her sight and telling no one. Assets: the Lantern Rites (true light-magic), the only reliable maps of the sealed doors, three centuries of records, and the moral trust of every village the Company has squeezed.
- **Agenda**: publicly, guard the open doors and heal what comes up. Actually: Kessilde is preparing to seize and re-seal the Great Stair itself, by force, before winter. She believes the Sundering was a divine quarantine and every open door is the wound reopening. She is closer to right than anyone knows, and her plan will kill Lanternwatch's economy and start a war with the Company.
- **Doctrine**: mercy at the door, steel at the threshold. The Vigil heals anyone, feeds anyone, and buries anyone, free. But on doctrine it does not negotiate, and it decides unilaterally what others are not ready to know.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: Kessilde's blindness is advancing and her likely successor, Preceptor Halden, is a zealot who would burn the stone-touched rather than quarantine them (social/succession); the order's founding vow, in the Aldermoor vault, reveals the first Vigil were not holy warriors but the dwarves' PAID gatekeepers, which would shatter doctrine (records/infiltration); the Lantern Rites draw on consecrated oil pressed at one grove, and the grove sits on land the Company just bought (economic, cross-faction).

## The Reclaimants

- **Who**: the dwarven diaspora's homecoming movement; ~300 sworn across the province plus sympathizers in every forge and quarry, led by **Thane Ulfra Deepsworn**, who has spent forty years assembling genealogies, door-keys, and grudges. Assets: tunnel-singers (the only people who can read the Deeproads' resonance), ancestral right (even the crown's law half-recognizes it), and the desperate cohesion of a people told for three centuries that their home is a graveyard.
- **Agenda**: retake Karag Vhol. Establish a foothold in the Upper Galleries, push to the Sunken Ring, relight the forges of the Hollow Crown. Ulfra knows the timing is suspicious and goes anyway, because her people are one generation from forgetting the songs.
- **Doctrine**: the long ledger. Reclaimants repay every debt, both kinds, across generations if needed. They will ally with anyone who helps them get home and forgive nothing done to stop them.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: the door-keys Ulfra collected are working TOO well, and she has begun to suspect the doors are opening FOR the Reclaimants, a thought she cannot afford (social/insight); her tunnel-singers report the deep resonance is a heartbeat and she has ordered them to stop saying so (suppressed knowledge); her son led the first foothold delve to the Middle Dark and has not reported in nine days as play opens (hook, see arcs.md).

## The Court Below (the fourth banner; secret tier)

- **Who/What**: surface rumor says "something opens the doors." GM truth: the Hollowed, the dwarves of Karag Vhol as the deep remade them, stone-fused, patient, still keeping their dead city to the rhythm of the Emberheart. Ruled by **Under-King Dror Vhol**, who sealed the gates three hundred years ago from the inside and has now unsealed them for one reason: something was STOLEN from the Gravemouth six years ago, and the Heart's quickening will not stop until it is returned. See `secrets.md` before running anything below the Upper Galleries.
- **Purpose**: the pack's antagonist, tragedy, and negotiating partner all at once. The Court Below is not a horde; it is a kingdom in mourning with infinite patience and terrible manners, and every one of its moves is, by its own lights, lawful recovery of stolen property.
