# HOLLOWCROWN - Secrets (GM)

The truths behind the campaign. Nothing here reaches the player except through play.

## What actually happened to Karag Vhol

The dwarves did not fall to an invasion. They dug too deep and into something that was ALWAYS there: the King Below, a buried titan older than the mountains, so vast that Karag Vhol's deepest works were tunnels in its bones. It is not dead and not alive. It DREAMS, slowly, and its dreaming seeps. The dwarves mined its marrow for three generations (the marrow is what made Vholic steel sing) before the seepage remade them: stone creeping through flesh, dreams replaced by the titan's dream, until a dwarf was a tending, patient, stone-fused thing. The Hollowed.

The Sundering was not a catastrophe. It was a DECISION. Under-King Dror Vhol, already half stone, understood what was happening and sealed every gate from the inside so it would never reach the surface. The seals were the dwarves' final act of love, and the surface built three centuries of ghost stories on it.

## The theft, and why the doors are open

Six years ago the Ledger Company's deep-survey (led by surveyor **Cass Werrin**, funded by Imra Sarn) breached a cracked side-door into the Gravemouth approaches and took core samples. One sample, a fist-sized shard of titan marrow, is the **Quiet Shard**. Its removal is a wound in the titan's dreaming: the Emberheart, the titan's slow pulse, has been QUICKENING ever since, one beat gaining on the next, year over year.

The quickening weakens the seals (the doors do not open; they FAIL), stirs the Hollowed from tending to acting, and leaks dream into the deep tiers (this is why nobody below dreams their own dreams). Dror Vhol has one goal: recover the Shard and return it to the Gravemouth, which he believes will slow the Heart. He is probably right. Nobody, including him, is certain.

**Where the Shard is now**: Sarn's vault in Lanternwatch, catalogued as "survey sample 7, inert." She knows the survey went somewhere it should not have; she does not know what the sample is. Cass Werrin knows more and drinks to keep from saying it.

## The Emberheart clock (the campaign's deep timer)

Track as a hidden campaign clock, ~90 in-game days at open: **[quickening 0/9]**, one tick per ~10 days or per major provocation (a death in the Gravemouth, marrow burned, the Shard damaged). At full: the Heart reaches waking rhythm, the mountains shrug, the Deeproads collapse or worse. This clock is meant to LOOM, not to fire; play should end it early, one way or another. The Archive Under Glass (below) is where the player can learn the real number.

## The Hollowed and the heralds

- Hollowed dwarves retain memory, law, and craft. They do not retain urgency, hunger, or fear. They speak Old Vholic and, slowly, remember common speech.
- They do not kill when recovery will do. The stone-touched caravans were QUESTIONED, below, about the theft (proximity to the deep marks the living; it fades over weeks, mostly).
- Escalation ladder if the Shard is not returned (see timeline): heralds sent up to ask, then to search, then to TAKE. A herald is a Hollowed lawspeaker with an honor guard; it walks into Lanternwatch reciting the theft's particulars in two languages and does not understand why everyone is screaming.
- Dror Vhol will negotiate, truthfully, with anyone who comes to the Sunken Ring under peace-sign. His terms are always the same: the Shard, returned, and the thieves named. What he will not do is wait forever.

## The Archive Under Glass (knowledge endgame)

In the Sunken Ring stands the Lawspeakers' Archive, sealed under a dome of dwarven glass, air-tight for three centuries. Inside: the last Lawspeaker's testament, written as the seals closed. It contains: what the titan is, what the marrow did, why the seals were mercy, the Gravemouth rites for returning what is taken, and the honest arithmetic of the Emberheart (how many beats remain at any quickening level). Whoever reads it gets the campaign's full truth in hard numbers, and the moral weight of choosing who else ever does. The Vigil would seal it forever; the Company would price it; the Reclaimants would grieve, then decide who they are.

## Resolution paths (seed all, force none)

- **Return the Shard**: the Gravemouth rites, a descent through everything, and a choice about who carries it (carrying it marks the carrier; see arcs). Slows the Heart, saves the seals, closes the shortcut economy, vindicates the Vigil, breaks the Reclaimants' homecoming unless they cut a deal below.
- **Destroy the Shard**: nobody knows what happens. Dror Vhol believes it wakes the titan outright. The Archive's testament contains the Lawspeaker's guess, and it is worse.
- **Wield it**: the Shard is a price-free wellspring for any magical art, and it is not free, it is DEFERRED (the wielder begins to Hollow, beautifully slowly; the sheet should whisper it in conditions before anyone says it aloud).
- **The Third Oath**: broker what Dror Vhol has never been offered: the living and the Hollowed keeping the deep TOGETHER, Reclaimants as wardens of the upper realm, the Vigil's doctrine rewritten by truth, the Company paying for all of it. The hardest path, needing leverage on all three surface factions plus trust below. It is the ending the pack is proudest of and the player must invent most of it.

## Infection-style rule the player learns the hard way

Deep-marking: hours spent below the Middle Dark accrue silently (GM tally). Thresholds: whispers of Old Vholic in sleep, then stone freckles, then shared dreaming (the player character starts perceiving Hollowed intentions, and vice versa: double-edged, genuinely useful, genuinely terrifying). It fades on the surface, slowly, except at the deepest threshold. Never state the mechanic; narrate it.
