# HOLLOWCROWN - Starting State (GM)

## Opening: Day 0, Lanternwatch, the hour the bells go wrong

The Great Stair's arrival bell rings the wrong pattern: not "caravan up," the old signal nobody has needed in three centuries: "seal watch to the gate." The player character is in the gate plaza when it happens (why they are there comes from creation).

Cold open with all three pressures landing inside the first scene:

1. **The caravan comes up wrong.** Fourteen souls, all alive, all stone-freckled, walking in perfect unhurried step, whispering in Old Vholic. The crowd's mood goes from market-day to riot-edge in minutes. Chainmen move to contain it quietly; a Vigil lantern-sergeant (Maren, first appearance) moves to protect the changed from the crowd; both are about to be wrong at each other.
2. **The carter dies choosing the player.** One carter, older, worse off than the rest, breaks step, grips the player's arm with granite-cold fingers, and presses a dwarven lodestone compass into their hand: "Doorkeeper now. It was ASKED for." Then the stone reaches his heart. First death of the campaign, on the player's boots, in front of everyone.
3. **Everyone saw.** A Chainman purser is already walking over with a receipt book ("Company evidence, citizen"). Maren saw. In the crowd, Brakka saw, and went very still. By nightfall, three factions know who holds the compass, and the player has not even had dinner.

First menu should offer (roughly): give it up to one of the approaching claimants, palm it and lie, run, ask the changed caravaners themselves, or something else. Every option is real; every option is WITNESSED.

## Character creation (interview, in-fiction framing)

Three questions, asked as the bell's echo dies and memory surfaces:

1. **Who were you before the Reach called you?** (trade seeds 2-3 starting traits and one tool; "called" allows both locals and newcomers)
2. **What do you carry that you cannot put down?** (an object, an oath, a debt, or a person: seeds one trait, one bond or hook, and the personal object rule)
3. **What has the dark already taken from you?** (seeds a scar: condition, reputation, or grief, and quietly tells the GM what this campaign is about for this player)

Aptitudes: assign the 4/3/3/2/2 spread from the answers (the trade places the 4). Reflect as fiction, never numbers. Magical arts at creation ONLY if the trade implies one (hearth-witch, lapsed lantern-novice, half-trained tunnel-singer), always with its price named in play early.

Starting inventory: travel clothes, one trade tool, one true weapon OR one art (not both), a purse that will not survive a week of Lanternwatch prices, flint, and one meaningful personal object (player defines; it will matter). Light: two candles and no torch, and make them feel it by nightfall.

## The Grayreach (surface gazetteer, GM reference)

- **Lanternwatch**: gate city over the Great Stair; Company charter town. Districts: the Stairhead (gatehouse, toll hall, Sarn's counting-house and vault), the Lantern Quarter (Vigil chapterhouse, hospice), Ropewalk (delver market, Fenn's territory), the Shambles (everyone the boom left behind).
- **Aldermoor**: barony town, Vigil motherhouse, two days east. Kessilde's seat; the founding-vow vault.
- **Harrow's End**: last village before the Thornfell; source of half the province's delvers and most of its funerals.
- **The Thornfell**: black pine wilds between settlements; outlaws, charcoal camps, and more sealed doors than any map admits.
- **The Drowned Door**: flooded Deeproad entrance at the fen's edge; smuggler economy, no tolls, no rescue. Cass Werrin's journal is hidden here.
- **The Worm Market**: neutral under-bazaar just inside the Upper Galleries where all three factions trade under truce-lamps; the campaign's crossroads and rumor engine.

## The Deeproads (tiers, GM reference)

- **Upper Galleries**: trade-lit, patrolled, almost safe; the shortcut economy lives here. Event ladder: tolls, rivals, cave-ins, pilgrims, the occasional door that was not open yesterday.
- **The Middle Dark**: past the truce-lamps; contested, unmapped in places, dream-leakage begins ("nobody dreams their own dreams"). Reclaimant foothold territory. Event ladder: silence that listens, waystations, marked delvers, Hollowed work-parties that do not acknowledge the living.
- **The Sunken Ring**: the Hollow Crown, capital of the Court Below, ringing the Crownpit. A functioning dead city: forges banked, looms kept, law recited. Entry is by peace-sign, invitation, or consequences. The Archive Under Glass stands here.
- **The Gravemouth**: below everything; the titan's marrow-works and the seat of the Emberheart. The endgame. Every hour here marks the living; the rites of return happen at its threshold.

Depth rules from pack.md apply: light counted, attention clocks per tier, retreat tracked as a resource, and the deep-marking tally from secrets.md.

## Board state at Day 0

- The compass: in the player's hand, warm, pointing down-and-east toward the Thornfell's nearest failing seal
- Company: containment first, acquisition second; Sarn hears "seal-warden's tool" and starts a file on the player by morning
- Vigil: Kessilde's seizure clock [0/5] already running; the wrong-pattern bell just accelerated her math
- Reclaimants: Ulfra is nine days into her son's silence and about to start asking outsiders; Brakka is already deciding whether the player can be trusted
- The Court Below: a herald is being prepared; first recitation at Lanternwatch in ~12 days
- The Emberheart: [quickening 0/9]; below the Middle Dark, the tunnel-singers' forbidden report is simply TRUE: the stone has a pulse
- Weather: high autumn, gold and cold; winter is the surface's own quiet clock, and the Vigil plans around it
