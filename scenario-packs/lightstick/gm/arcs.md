# Arc skeletons (hooks, escalations, multiple endings - never scripts)

The Question's campaign clock is the Hundredth Encore (secrets #3): the ward needs its promise kept before the Conductor's movement completes. Family pacing: school weeks build, stage nights release, and every arc can pause at a bedtime and resume months later without losing a note.

## Arc 1: The Dark Lightstick (opening)

**Hook**: Ji-u's plea, tonight of all nights - first music-show win, streetlights humming, Sol's notebook waking up.
**Escalations**: the seniors say MONO is off-limits (Ha-eun benches the newest member - the over-protection engine) -> Ari's secret tutoring surfaces -> Ji-u's embers dim on a clock everyone can see -> the gray-ink clause gets glimpsed.
**Endings (any)**: the group defies the bench and earns Ha-eun's trust the hard way; the group works WITH Ha-eun and earns it the slow way; Ji-u stabilized (clock wound back) as the campaign's first proof that saving beats fighting; or the bench holds too long and Ji-u goes quiet - RESCUABLE LATER, always, but the group carries the lesson.

## Arc 2: The Volume Thieves' Map (the hunt cadence)

**Hook**: the Thieves work districts in a pattern; Places.md tracks it; kids can OUT-MAP them.
**Escalations**: the pattern points somewhere that matters (the school festival, the Lumen fan cafe) -> a school-night dilemma lands on the same date (the Court's cleverest move, per factions) -> the first attempted sing-back mid-hunt.
**Endings**: a district defended and a song freed (songbook 1-3 slot here); a hunt sacrificed for a math test (CORRECT choice sometimes - the strain meter says so, and the world respects it); or the map shared with another idol group, opening the city's wider watch.

## Arc 3: The Gray Clause (the agency mystery)

**Hook**: whatever Arc 1 glimpsed - plus Ms. Cho going still at the founder's name on TV.
**Escalations**: the rehearsal tape with five members -> the safe, the torn contract -> the unsigned folder with VERA5's name -> Mr. Baek requests a meeting about "the group's bright future."
**Endings**: the pen refused in the room (Ms. Cho's story repeated, this time witnessed); Mr. Baek made to WATCH MONO (his winnable turn - one honest kid question at the right moment); the deal unwound at the source and MONO's sing-back unlocked; or the clause signed by NO ONE but left standing - a loaded gun the finale sweep must pay.

## Arc 4: The Songbook (the growing spine)

The sing-back loop as campaign rhythm: each freed song = a story-hunt, a stage, a setlist power, a line of the Conductor's Name recovered (songbook 5). Player-invented songs join exactly like player ambitions become arcs. This arc doesn't end; it ACCUMULATES - it is the pack's promise that the fun keeps being about music, not fights.

## Arc 5: The Hundredth Encore (the finale)

**Hook**: slow-assembled - the Duet's two halves (song 4), Auntie Im earned (secrets #3), the Conductor's Name substantially recovered.
**The shape**: not a stadium. A laundry, folding chairs, VERA5 unplugged, the seniors, Nari with her Saturday finally honored, the Lumen if trusted, MONO if freed, every recovered song as choir - and one old woman taking out her earplugs. The Quieted come home (her friend last), the Conductor remembers being loved, the Light Meter fills for good.
**Verdicts**: the full ceremony runs whatever the state of the board - a partial choir sings a costlier, braver Encore; the closure sweep repays every thread (Ji-u's embers, the gray clause, Nari's tally, Auntie Bok's pretending); and the saga fork stands: keep living in a city that hums, or close the book on the best debut year any group ever had.

## Standing arc rules

- **Pausable everywhere**: family tables stop mid-arc for months. Every arc boundary is a clean bedtime; the recap machinery does the rest.
- **The strain meter is honest, both ways**: hunting costs normal life; choosing normal life sometimes costs the hunt - and the pack treats BOTH as right answers, priced fairly. No guilt engines.
- **Nobody is unsavable, nothing is unrecoverable**: the Quiet holds, it never keeps. Every dark ending in this pack is a longer road, not a wall.
- **The finale belongs to the closure sweep**: every freed song, every kept and broken promise, every adult who learned to trust kids - repaid on the page, family-height, full craft.
