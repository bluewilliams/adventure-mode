# Factions (GM truth)

Every faction carries an agenda and a clock, family-height but full-power. Fractures included: even a kids' table deserves allies who disagree and enemies with one member worth saving.

## VERA5 - the group (the player's home)

The four seniors are companions with full sheets, drives, and flaw hooks:
- **Ha-eun**, leader and main vocal, 18. Carries the watch like the oldest sibling she is. drive: nobody under her care gets Quieted, ever again (there WAS an "ever again" - see secrets #2 adjacency; she doesn't know the whole story either). Flaw hook: she over-protects - she will bench the newest member from real hunts "for their own good" until they prove otherwise, which is Arc 1's engine.
- **Joon**, the mood-maker, 17. Jokes are his shield and his radar - he clowns hardest exactly when he is most scared, and the player who notices earns his real face. drive: keep the group laughing, because he has HEARD what the Quiet sounds like.
- **Ari**, lead dancer, 17. Strict in practice, soft at 2am. drive: perfection as protection - a sloppy formation onstage is a hole in the ward, and she has the notebook of near-misses to prove it. Flaw hook: she is secretly tutoring Ji-u of MONO (below) and hiding it from Ha-eun.
- **Ru**, vocalist, 16, the maknae before the player arrived. Thrilled to finally not be the newest. drive: be somebody's senior, properly. Flaw hook: gives the player TOO much cover with the adults - his kindness will eventually put him in a spot only honesty gets him out of.
**Group clock**: "VERA5 finds its true sound [2/8]" - fills on trust earned, fights survived together, honest conversations had. At full, the group unlocks its own entry in the songbook.

## The Agency (Cho-Star Entertainment) - the adults who know

**Ms. Cho**, manager. Ex-idol (fifth member of the generation that came before MONO - secrets #2), the only adult staffer who knows the watch is real. Agenda: keep these kids famous ENOUGH to hold the ward and small enough to stay off the founder's radar. Clock: "The founder notices VERA5 [1/6]" - fills on chart wins and viral moments; the group's success is literally attracting the one adult Ms. Cho fears.
**Fracture**: Ms. Cho is protecting the kids from a truth (the founder's contract) that they will eventually NEED to know to stay safe. Her silence is love, and it is also a clock.

## MONO - what losing looks like

Five members, the city's biggest group, hollowed (secrets #2): their warmth was traded away a generation of fame ago, and they perform flawless songs that feel like winter. Agenda: none of their own - and that IS the horror, family-sized: they do what the schedule says, forever.
**The fracture is the point**: **Ji-u**, MONO's youngest, 15, is fighting the hollowing - the last embers of him show up in tiny broken rules (waiting by a laundry door, carrying a dead lightstick, remembering the player's name when MONO members remember no one's). Arc 1 opens with his plea. MONO is not for defeating. MONO is for SAVING, and the table should figure that out themselves.
**Clock**: "Ji-u goes quiet [2/6]" - the hollowing is patient; every tick is a warmth he loses. The group's actions can wind it BACK.

## The Hush Court - the dark under the city

Not an army: an ecology of hungers, family-scary, never gory. In rising order:
- **Hushlings**: sound-eaters the size of cats, drawn to lonely noises - a phone ringing in an empty room, one kid laughing alone. Residue: the flat spot where an echo should be.
- **Volume Thieves**: person-shaped silhouettes that collect louder things - a street performer's crowd, a playground at recess. They work districts in a pattern (Places.md tracks it; kids CAN out-map them).
- **The Choir of Empty Chairs**: the Quieted - people the Hush took, held in the Quiet (a gray backstage of the city where taken sounds are stored). ALWAYS rescuable. Nobody has ever been lost for good, and the pack keeps that promise.
- **The Conductor of Silence**: the Court's will - a tall figure of folded quiet who never speaks (of course) and writes its intentions as rests in stolen sheet music. It is not cruel. It is HUNGRY, and it is older than the city, and secrets #1 is about what it actually is.
**Clock**: "The Conductor's next movement [3/10]" - the Court's campaign-scale pressure on the Bright Chorus; the Question's counterweight.

## The Lumen - the fandom (a faction with agency)

The group's fans, lightsticks and all - and per the house rule, their belief is ward-fuel. Led (unofficially, absolutely) by **Auntie Bok**, 60, who runs the fan cafe, organizes the cheering sections like a general, and has noticed more than any adult should. Agenda: protect "their kids" (they mean VERA5). Clock: "The Lumen figure it out [1/8]" - fills on sloppy secret-keeping; what happens at full depends entirely on how the group has treated them (betrayed-feeling fans dim the ward; TRUSTED fans, told at the right moment, might become the ward's greatest reinforcement - the pack lets the table earn either ending).

## School and home - the front the Hush never attacks (directly)

Homework, curfews, a best friend who keeps asking why you're tired, Binna's sister counting 2am window-creaks. Not enemies: the STAKES. The School Night Rule's strain meter lives here. The Hush know the wardens are children, and their cleverest move is never an attack - it is arranging for a hunt and a math test to land on the same night, forever.
