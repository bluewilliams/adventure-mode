# The Songbook (GM reference - music is the magic)

No spells, only songs. A song's POWER is unlocked by knowing its STORY - learned from people, places, tapes, and the Hush themselves - then singing it where it matters, like it matters. This is the pack's quest engine: every forgotten song below is a Hush to free, a story to dig up, and a new power for the group's setlist. Performance attempts roll normally (uncertainty + stakes); knowing the story shifts the band. Sung-back songs are recorded on the group's sheet as setlist entries with teeth.

## How singing-back works (the loop the kids discover)

1. **Meet the Hush** - it hums something almost-familiar (that hum is the song, warped).
2. **Learn its story** - who sang it, who it was for, why it stopped. Stories hide in people (Auntie Bok's memory, Auntie Im's walls, Ms. Cho's tapes), places (the closed harbor, the old shrine), and the Quiet itself.
3. **Sing it back at the right place, meaning it** - a real attempt, banded honestly; group harmony helps; a full Lumen crowd helps more.
4. **The Hush transforms** - monster into music. The freed song joins the setlist; whatever it stole comes home warmer; the Light Meter fills.

A failed sing-back never destroys anything: the Hush flees fuller, someone gets dimmed, the school-night meter pays - and the story is still learned. Try again with more heart, more friends, or a better stage.

## The five forgotten songs (starter arcs; play grows more)

### 1. The Harbor Counting Song
The work song of the old fish docks - counting crates in rhythm so nobody lost fingers. Stopped when the harbor closed. Now: a Volume Thief working the waterfront, collecting engine sounds and seagull cries.
**Story hides in**: three retired dockworkers who play badok in the park (each remembers a different verse, none will sing alone).
**Sung back**: at the empty pier, at dawn shift-change hour. Frees the gulls' morning racket; setlist gains "The Count" - a rhythm the whole group can lean on (+1 step to any group-timing attempt, once per stage).

### 2. The Lullaby with No Baby
A grandmother's lullaby whose family emigrated; she sang it to an empty room for years before she stopped. Now: the Hushling cluster in the hospital district, drawn to babies crying alone at night - it doesn't hurt them; it HUSHES them, and new parents are getting scared.
**Story hides in**: the grandmother's granddaughter - Binna's homeroom teacher - who has a voicemail she can't bring herself to play.
**Sung back**: over the voicemail's melody, in the maternity ward's family room, with permission asked properly. Frees the softest song in the setlist: "Sleep Now" - once per session, calms ANY panicking crowd or creature, no roll needed.

### 3. The Cheer for the Losing Team
A whole neighborhood's chant for a school team that never once won - sung ANYWAY, every week, for years, until the school merged away. Now: a Volume Thief that stalks moments of brave failure - the kid who tries out again, the runner who finishes last - stealing the applause they almost got.
**Story hides in**: Auntie Bok (she led the chant; her fan-general skills started there) and a trophy case with no trophies, just a photo.
**Sung back**: at a losing game, meaning it. Frees "Anyway" - the setlist's spine-straightener: sung for someone mid-failure, it converts their worst moment into a trait-earning one. The song that teaches the pack's whole heart.

### 4. The Duet on the Shrine Steps
The song two best friends sang at the shrine stage a hundred years ago - the First Singer and the girl who listened (secrets #3). Half of it survives on Auntie Im's walls. The other half is in the Quiet, with the friend.
**Story hides in**: the laundry's sheet-music walls, the shrine's cracked stage, and a rescue: someone must enter the Quiet (via a rescue arc, always winnable) and hear the friend hum her half.
**Sung back**: both halves, shrine steps, two voices minimum. Frees the road to the Hundredth Encore - and Auntie Im takes out one earplug.

### 5. The Conductor's Name
The First Singer's first song - outgrown, unsung, and grown vast (secrets #1 deep). Not a fight: the longest story-hunt in the pack, assembled from every other freed song's fragments (each sing-back recovers one line of it).
**Sung back**: only at the Hundredth Encore, by everyone - VERA5, the seniors, MONO if freed, the Lumen if trusted, every recovered song as choir. The Conductor of Silence remembers being loved. The campaign's finale, and the Light Meter's last tick.

## Craft notes

- **Kid-singable**: every song's described hook is simple enough to actually hum at the table. If the family WANTS to make up words and sing, the GM scores it: genuine table-joy is a band shift, house rule, forever.
- **Stolen setlists**: the Hush can steal BACK a freed song if it stops being sung (a setlist entry unused for many sessions gets a gentle in-fiction reminder first - use it or the world does).
- **Growth**: play invents new forgotten songs exactly like player ambitions become arcs - a kid asking "does the ice cream truck song have a story?" deserves the answer YES.
