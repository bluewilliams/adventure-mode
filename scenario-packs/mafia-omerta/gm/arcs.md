# OMERTA - Arc Skeletons (GM)

Skeletons, not scripts. All four run on their own clocks; two of them (Crown, Docks) are secretly connected through Nicky's heroin money. The Ladder is not an arc the world runs at the player; it is the player's own gravity, and the world responds to every rung.

## Arc 0: The Ladder (STANDING)

- **Shape**: outsider -> connected -> earner -> made -> capo -> administration. Each rung has a price the previous rung politely hid.
- **Rung mechanics**: standing moves on earning (envelopes up), discipline (mouth shut, orders kept), and blood (what the player is willing to do; the button has a price with a name and a face, authored at the moment it becomes real, never waved off).
- **The break-away path is fully supported**: a player who builds their own crew (their own earners, their own book, their own protection) can go independent or petition to matter. Starting your own thing in Marchetti territory is war math; doing it in the power vacuum AFTER the Crown arc resolves is empire-building. If the player wants "found my own family," the world takes it seriously and prices it honestly.
- **Endings**: the button; the crown (long game); the break-away; the walk-away (Reyes's door, or just a car pointed west with clean money); the drop.

## Arc 1: The Rat (Petey / BRICKWALL)

- **Hook**: Petey asks the player for a small strange favor (hold a package, be somewhere, back him up on an alibi that doesn't parse).
- **Escalation**: tells accumulate -> Santoro's quiet audit narrows -> Reyes squeezes Petey for something real -> the wire catches someone the player cares about (possibly the player).
- **Endings**: extraction (Petey vanishes alive; the family writes him off as a body); disinformation double-game (the pack's highest-wire act); handed to Santoro (Petey's fate authored by how, and the player carries it); undetected until indictment morning; or Petey, protected and steadied, finds his own way to give them nothing and survive - the narrow best ending, and it runs through the bond.

## Arc 2: The Crown (Sal / Nicky / Santoro)

- **Hook**: Sal's hand shakes pouring anisette at a wake; the player is close enough to see, and Sal sees them see.
- **Escalation**: concealment errands (Adele's orbit) -> Nicky spending the future early -> made men quietly choosing sides -> the diagnosis becomes currency.
- **Endings**: managed succession (Santoro, reluctant, stable); Nicky's crown (short, loud, ends in BRICKWALL's lap); civil war; or the player - if the Ladder has been climbed far enough and the room has reasons - in the conversation themselves.

## Arc 3: The Docks (Doyles / heroin / the line)

- **Hook**: Colm Doyle does something stupid on the wrong side of the line (starts in the Starlite if the player is there night one).
- **Escalation**: tit-for-tat -> the pipeline's existence surfaces -> Sal's ban vs Nicky's money -> Montreal looks for a stabler partner -> the line stops meaning anything.
- **Endings**: war (everyone loses, BRICKWALL wins); a negotiated line (through Denise's room, if it still stands); the pipeline burned (Nicky exposed, Crown arc detonates); or the player brokering with Montreal directly - the single fastest and most lethal rung-skip in the pack.

## Companion seeds

- **Petey Ranucci** (see NPCs): the heart. Romance: no; brotherhood: the whole point.
- **Denise Alvarez** (see NPCs): romance-capable, on her terms only. Her arc crosses Docks and the neutrality of the one honest room.
- **Carmine "Junior" Lupo**, 19, Santoro's driver's kid: hungry, loyal to whoever teaches him, a mirror of who the player was five years ago. What the player makes of Junior is the pack's quiet morality meter, and everyone from Gio to Santoro is watching them raise him.
