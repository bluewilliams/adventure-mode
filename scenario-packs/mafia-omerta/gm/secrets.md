# OMERTA - GM Secrets

SPOILERS. Player-facing text must never contain unflipped facts from this file.

## The rat (the pack's beating heart)

**Petey Ranucci is cooperating with the FBI.** Five weeks ago a task force detective caught him with a trunk full of untaxed cigarettes and a parole-adjacent gun charge; Operation BRICKWALL flipped him in a night. He has given them small things - a fence's address, two names they already had - and is stalling like a man treading water. He has not given them the player. He tells himself he never will.

- His handler, **Agent Dana Reyes**, is patient, smart, and running out of patience from above.
- Petey's tells (discoverable): new payphone habits, a car he "borrowed" that's too clean, begging off jobs he'd have jumped at, drinking at bars on the WRONG side of the line where nobody knows him.
- Resolution space is wide open: catch it early and get him out of town; feed disinformation through him; turn him into a double; hand him to Santoro (and live with what happens); or never see it, and count the cost at indictment time. His bond with the player bends every branch.

## Operation BRICKWALL

Federal task force (FBI + IRS) working the Marchetti family under the new RICO playbook. Currently: one live wire on the Bellavista Social Club's back-room phone (installed via the phone company two months ago), Petey, and a financial squeeze building on the family's union pension skim.

- The wire is the quiet killer: anything said on that phone is on tape. Santoro is old-school careful; Nicky Tempesta is not.
- **Timeline if untouched**: sealed indictments in ~6 months naming Nicky, two capos, and (depending on what the player has said near that phone) possibly the player.

## The crown

**Sal Marchetti is dying.** Parkinson's, concealed as "his hip," maybe two good years left. Succession is the family's unexploded bomb:

- Nicky Tempesta assumes the crown is his and has started spending like it.
- Sal privately believes Nicky will destroy the family and favors **Santoro**, who does not want it (that is why Sal wants him).
- Sal's true confidant is his lawyer, **Adele Katz**, consigliere in everything but title; she knows the diagnosis, the will, and where thirty years of bodies are buried, figuratively and not.

## The heroin question

Sal's ban on narcotics is real and absolute. **Nicky is already in**: he has quiet money in a heroin pipeline moving through the Doyle-controlled docks, brokered by the Doyles' Montreal supplier. If Sal learns, Nicky dies. If the feds learn, everyone dies slower. Two arcs (Crown, Docks) are secretly the same arc because of this.

## Uncle Gio

Gio was shelved in 1961 because he refused an order from young Sal Marchetti: to kill a friend who turned out to be innocent of what he was accused of. Sal shelved him instead of killing him, an old debt of respect. Gio knows where the body of that era's REAL informant is, and who put it there. His silence is the price of his life; his knowledge is a skeleton key to the old guard. `known: false` at the deepest level; unlocks only through earned family trust.

## The paint on this world's door

There is no cure for what the player is walking into: the family is a dying institution that eats its young, wearing loyalty like a Sunday suit. The pack never says this out loud. Every arc ending quietly asks whether the player figured it out in time to choose something else, or climbed all the way up before looking down.
