# OMERTA - Starting State (GM)

## Opening: Friday, October 12, 1979, 6:40 PM, Brickport

Golden hour on Adler Ave. The player character has been hanging around the edge of Santoro's crew for a year: errands, lookout work, an envelope here and there. Tonight is different. Tonight **Santoro asked for them by name.**

Cold open with three pressures that all want the same evening:

1. **The job.** Santoro, at his corner table in the Bellavista: a collection. **Vic Muraro**, appliance-store owner, four weeks behind on a twelve-grand note, stopped answering the phone. Bring back the money, or bring back the reason. First real test: how the player handles Vic (broke, scared, and hiding something: the store's books are cooked to cover his son's habit) writes their reputation's first line.
2. **The favor.** Petey catches the player outside the club, too casual: needs them to hold a set of car keys "for a couple days, don't ask, it's nothing." (It is not nothing: the keys belong to a task-force loaner he was given and is terrified to be seen in. First thread of Arc 1.)
3. **The bar.** At the Starlite tonight, three Doyle guys drinking on the wrong side of the line, including **Colm Doyle**, getting louder. Denise hasn't asked anyone for anything in eleven years. Tonight she catches the player's eye and holds it one second too long.

First menu should offer (roughly): head straight to Vic's before he rabbits, take Petey's keys / press him / refuse, plant yourself at the Starlite, walk the long way and think (gather info), or something else. The evening has room for two of the three, not all. What gets left unattended tonight becomes Monday's problem, with interest.

## Character creation (interview, in-fiction framing)

Three questions, asked as the player character walks Adler Ave toward the club at golden hour:

1. **How are you connected?** (blood, neighborhood, a favor owed, army buddy of a made guy: seeds standing nuance and who claims them)
2. **What do you bring that the family wants?** (driving, numbers, fists, a trade, a face people trust: seeds 2-3 starting traits and the flavor of early jobs)
3. **What line won't you cross - and what have you already done?** (seeds a moral anchor the game WILL test, one scar or debt, and the character's private clock)

Aptitudes: assign the 4/3/3/2/2 spread from the answers (what they bring the family places the 4). Reflect as fiction, never numbers; Santoro's crew will discover what the player is good at the same way the player does, by watching.

Starting inventory: decent clothes, a watch worth pawning, $340 cash, keys to a borrowed Monte Carlo (Uncle Gio's), and one meaningful personal object (player defines; it will matter). A gun ONLY if the player asks for one, with the house-rule weight stated in-fiction by whoever provides it.

Starting contacts: seed player-facing `NPCs/` notes for Petey, Santoro, Denise, Frank Bell, and Uncle Gio (brief-level facts only). Petey is available as an early companion; his Arc 1 makes that closeness a loaded gun.

Starting STANDING: **connected** (known to the crew, claimed by nobody, worth nothing yet).
