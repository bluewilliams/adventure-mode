# Arc skeletons (hooks, escalations, multiple endings - never scripts)

The Question's campaign clock is generational: the debt's forbearance runs out at "the next great Reckoning" - years away, its true deadline discoverable (the Weather-Debt Tally), arriving regardless. Every arc feeds the Question; none answers it alone. Seasons pace everything: raids at Raidtide, hunger in the Long Dark, politics at the year-moots, and the Reckoning every year's end (settle the ledger, pay the tithe, read the omens).

## Arc 1: The land-taking (the first year)

**Hook**: the claim is staked; now it must HOLD through four seasons - Vosk's test, Ittra's offers, hay-arithmetic, and a barrow-field nobody else wanted (now you know why, and also do not).
**Escalations**: Ragnvald's welcome visit -> a boundary insult engineered to measure response -> the first Raidtide choice (target, tribute, or nerve) -> the Long Dark's true test (hay-days vs herd vs Mool's hungry eyes).
**Endings (any)**: hold with nerve (Vosk respect, a raid survived or deterred); hold by weave (Ittra marriage/loan, with its clauses); hold by feeding Mool (the cheapest spear-wall in the Vale, bought with respect); fail forward (ground lost, clan intact, a grudge ledgered) - or break (the succession rule fires in year one, and the saga is ALREADY about the heir).

## Arc 2: The names in the ground (the mystery's engine)

**Hook**: the door-stone woman's trade (starting-state): a name for a truth. The barrow-field pays attention to how it is treated.
**Escalations**: first recovered name (Kilnborn purchase, rook contract, or Hakon's lullaby recognized) -> first FREEING (one hour of century-old testimony; secrets #1 middle depth) -> Halla's Deepwalk unlocks safe parley -> the hidden clock: every freeing thins the escrow, and the WEATHER begins responding to namings (storms that circle the barrow-field without breaking; Sæunn hears the soil's lie changing pitch).
**Endings**: the archivist path (systematic recovery: the clan becomes the Vale's memory, with all the power and targets that paints); the trader path (names as currency to the Court, the Kilnborn, and desperate clans - lucrative, corrosive, and the dead NOTICE being retailed); the closed path (seal the field, refuse the whole question - the Vale's politics still come, but the mystery arrives by other doors, later and worse-informed).

## Arc 3: The moot and the tribe-making (the Vale decides its shape)

**Hook**: Aud calls the first full moot in a hundred years - ostensibly over drove-road law; actually (her steering) to make the clans a TRIBE before crown-fever does it badly.
**Escalations**: multi-round negotiation with real demands on the table - moot seats, raid-shares, the Graywater tolls, fosterages, who feeds Mool, WHO SITS THE EMPTY CHAIR; Sigrun maneuvers the rite onto the agenda; Stig floats "settling the crown question" and three clans' clocks lurch.
**Endings**: a tribe with the player central (won by the season's actual conduct, not speeches); a tribe with the player peripheral (survivable; influence must be rebuilt); no tribe (the Vale stays kindling and Raidtide finds it); or the player's OWN design - some tables will invent a structure Aud never imagined, and the pack's law (Erda's) is that another way is always allowed to work.

## Arc 4: The crown-race (the bomb in the barrow)

**Hook**: Hallgerd Sarn is dying. Sigrun's rite-clock fills. Stig starts shopping. Three fuses, one instrument.
**Escalations**: the deathbed choice (the trust recited to ONE person of character); Gest's "hoddgild" afternoon; the Tally surfacing in Kilnborn inventory at a price that signals they know exactly what it is; a rival (Dreng's rite or Stig's buyer or an ambitious player) reaching the crown-barrow's approaches, where Sarn's watchers and the Court's oldest dead hold the line.
**Endings**: the crown raised UNKNOWING (by anyone, including the player: coronation, a golden year, then the remainder called at the Reckoning - the catastrophe run honestly, and the saga continues in its ruins); the crown raised KNOWING (someone assumes the debt deliberately, with a repayment plan - the boldest legitimate play in the pack); the crown withheld (Sarn's trust honored and shored up for another generation: a holding pattern, bought with politics); or the debt addressed FIRST (arcs 2+5 material: pay-down, Third Answer, or Pleading), after which the crown is just a crown, and the Vale may finally, safely, argue about who wears it.

## Arc 5: The Reckoning cycle (the yearly spine)

**Hook**: every year's end: settle the seasonal ledger, then the tithe decision - what the clan pays deliberately, and what it hoards.
**Escalations**: the Storm-court's heat as a visible pattern across years (paid clans get weather; hoarding, oath-breaking clans get WEATHER); rivals' tithe-craft becomes legible intelligence (Vosk under-pays and dares the sky; Dreng over-pays and starves its levy; Ittra pays in OTHER PEOPLE'S goods); the great-Reckoning deadline emerges from the Tally as a real year with a real number.
**Endings**: this arc doesn't end; it PACES - it is the pack's heartbeat, the settle_ops ritual dressed in religion, and the reason a ten-year saga never has a dead season. Its one terminal state belongs to the finale below.

## The Finale (three lawful answers to the Question, plus the honest fourth)

1. **Repayment**: generations of deliberate tithe-craft plus Erda's Third Answer retire the remainder without ruin. The crown becomes safe; whoever raises it - the player's line or an ally earned - inherits a Vale, not a debt. The closure sweep pays every name freed along the way.
2. **The Pleading**: the Unnamed's Deepwalk, won on evidence the whole campaign gathered: over-collection proven, remainder voided, escrow ordered open. Every name returns AT ONCE - the counter-Silence, a valley remembering itself in a single night - and Aud's surety ends, which she meets however the table's Aud has grown to meet it.
3. **Repetition**: the debt called, unpaid, at the great Reckoning - or the crown raised unknowing at the worst hour. A second Silence, EARNED, never imposed: the campaign's clocks all said so. The Long Epilogue tells what the next century's returners find, and whose descendants they are.
4. **The walked-away**: some chiefs will take the clan OUT of the Vale rather than gamble it. Legitimate, priced (the clan that leaves keeps its lives and abandons its dead - now knowing whose dead they are), and the world-momentum finale answers the Question without them.

## Standing arc rules

- **Generations are the medium**: chiefs age, sicken, and die (the body keeps score; childbed and winter are honest dice). Grooming an heir IN PLAY - a real character with drives, taught things, present at councils - is the difference between succession and civil war. The saga's finest chapters are often the second chief's.
- **Counsel runs everything**: every major decision arrives with the council's takes from their files. The advisor whose warnings keep proving right accrues weight the table can FEEL; so does the one whose god keeps getting stiffed at the Reckoning.
- **Culture is enforced by consequences, never lectures**: weregild refused, guest-right strained, oaths under sky - the world reacts through law, doors, and weather. Nobody ever says "that's against the rules." The Vale just responds.
- **The Silence never gets a villain**: no cult, no dark lord, no monologue. A contract, its author, its collateral, and its heirs. Keep it that way; the pack's dread lives in the paperwork being REAL.
- **The finale belongs to the closure sweep**: whichever ending - crowned, voided, silenced, or walked away - every freed name, every oath under sky, every council grudge, and Aud's century are repaid on the page.
