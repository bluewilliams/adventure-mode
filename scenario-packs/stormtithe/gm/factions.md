# Factions (GM truth)

Every clan runs at the outfit grain (Running at Scale): instantiate each with units, a seasonal ledger, an agenda, and a clock at game start. Ledger period = the SEASON. Numbers are first-Sowing truth; the player holds only estimates. The five clans are archetypes with insides: each has a public face, a private fracture, and a price.

## Clan Vosk - strength is the tithe

**Agenda**: take. Raiding is theology: the strong collect the tithe the weak owe the gods anyway. Ragnvald wants the Vale acknowledged as his herd, and the crown-talk irritates him - crowns are for people who need permission.
**Clock**: "Vosk rides at Raidtide [0/4]" - resets every year at Sowing; fills toward the season's first raid target, chosen by weakness shown. The player's claim starts as the OBVIOUS target.
**Units**: sworn riders | leader: Ragnvald | strength 24 | quality veteran | loyalty: solid | loadout: spears, seaxes, 6 horses fit for war. Shield-kin | strength 40 | quality seasoned | loyalty: solid. Thralls-of-war | strength 15 | quality green | loyalty: none owed | tags: watching for a better master.
**Ledger truth**: ~14 cattle-worth/season (herds, tribute from two cowed hill-steads, raid-shares); costs low, iron poor - Vosk WEALTH is horses and fear, and fear pays badly in the Long Dark.
**Fracture**: Ragnvald's heir, Eyvor, thinks raiding returning clans is skinning the herd you should be milking - she counts what tribute would yield versus what raids cost and she is RIGHT. She will not move against her father. She will listen.
**Disposition**: tests everyone once, early, personally. Respects a bloodied nose more than a bent knee; despises groveling; keeps his word to the LETTER and hunts loopholes in yours.

## Clan Ittra - everything crosses our bridge

**Agenda**: bind. Marriages, tolls, loans, fostered children - Ittra wins the Vale by being married to it. War is a market failure.
**Clock**: "The Ittra web [0/10]" - each segment is a clan bound by marriage, debt, or fosterage. At full, no moot can convene without Ittra consent.
**Units**: bridge-wards | strength 18 | quality seasoned | loyalty: solid | doing: the Graywater crossings. Pack-trains | strength 25 | quality green | doing: the drove roads, guarded weakly on purpose (robbing Ittra means owing Ittra).
**Ledger truth**: ~20 cattle-worth/season (tolls, loans, pack-train margins) - the Vale's richest book, and the most encumbered (below).
**SECRET LEDGER TRUTH**: Ittra is the Kilnborn's largest debtor by far - three generations of trade-goods advanced against "future considerations" nobody living has read the fired-clay terms of. Matriarch Unn Ittra suspects the debt is the kind you pay in something other than goods and is quietly marrying her family OUT of the direct line of obligation (see secrets.md #3 adjacency).
**Disposition**: arrives with gifts, a marriage sketch, and a loan offer scaled to your desperation. Genuinely useful, never free.

## Clan Dreng - the worthy shall raise the crown

**Agenda**: sanctify. Sigrun Chief-Priestess preaches that the Silence was the gods' judgment on an unworthy king, and the Vale will only be safe under a WORTHY one - crowned by rite, at the king-stone, by her.
**Clock**: "The Rite of Worth [0/12]" - segments are omens confirmed, temples raised, clans pledged to Dreng's crowning rite. At full, Sigrun declares the rite regardless of who is ready, and the crown question goes from politics to theology.
**Units**: temple-guard | strength 20 | quality seasoned | loyalty: to the temple, not Sigrun. Pilgrim-levy | strength 50 | quality green | loyalty: shaky | tags: fed by the temple, there for the bread.
**Ledger truth**: ~11 cattle-worth/season (temple tithes, pilgrim gifts, good mid-vale pasture) - and the pilgrim-levy eats most of it; Dreng is one bad Harvest from choosing between bread and piety.
**Fracture**: Sigrun's own law-speaker, old Gest, has read the temple's one pre-Silence rune-post honestly and believes the opposite of the sermon: the crown is not a reward, it is a LIABILITY (he is nearly right - secrets.md #2). He drinks. Nobody asks why anymore.
**Disposition**: warm to the pious, glacial to the impious, and desperate - beneath the certainty - for ANY genuine sign from the gods, which the player's barrow-field may start producing.

## Clan Mool - the many

**Agenda**: eat. Twice the mouths of any clan, half the pasture; Mool's chief, Brekka, does arithmetic all night and hates every answer. Mool follows bread. Mool remembers who gave it and who priced it.
**Clock**: "Mool breaks [0/6]" - ticks on hungry seasons and insults; at full, Mool splinters into raiding bands and every clan's herds bleed (the Vale's powder keg - a generous player can WIND IT BACK, the only clock in the pack that visibly runs both ways).
**Units**: the gray levy | strength 70 | quality green | loyalty: to bread | tags: hungry. Brekka's own | strength 8 | quality veteran | loyalty: solid.
**Ledger truth**: ~9 cattle-worth/season against the Vale's biggest upkeep - Mool runs a structural deficit every Long Dark, and Brekka knows the exact size of the hole to the half-sheep.
**Disposition**: proud, prickly, impossible to buy cheaply because charity insults them - but FEED THEM WITH RESPECT (work, trade, fosterage, a place at the feast) and Mool is the loyalest spear-wall in the Vale.

## Clan Sarn - the old blood

**Agenda**: wait, then claim. The Sarn descent from the last High King is TRUE, they hold the location of the crown-barrow (their founding trust), and their whole creed is that the crown must NOT be raised until "the debt is paid" - words their own young no longer understand (their elders barely do; secrets.md #2).
**Clock**: "Sarn runs out of time [0/8]" - old Chief Hallgerd is dying; her heir, Stig, thinks the family creed is superstition and the crown is leverage. When she dies unconvinced-of-an-alternative, Stig starts quietly shopping the crown-barrow's location.
**Units**: high-pasture kin | strength 22 | quality veteran | loyalty: solid | tags: silent by policy. The watchers | strength 6 | quality veteran | doing: the crown-barrow approaches, always, in all weather.
**Ledger truth**: ~12 cattle-worth/season (high pasture, wool, quiet Kilnborn iron-trade nobody else gets those prices on) - modest spending, deep reserves; Sarn could outlast a siege of years and everyone suspects it.
**Disposition**: courteous, closed, and watchful. The one clan that will never discuss the Silence - because their founding trust is a fragment of the truth, transmitted without its context, and they know how mad it sounds.

## Blind Aud and the moot-stone circuit

Not a clan: the Vale's law-speaker, keeper of the moot circuit, adviser to all, member of none. TRUTH: see secrets.md #3 - Aud is a century older than she can admit, was law-speaker to the last High King, drafted the bargain that became the Silence, and cannot die until the debt she wrote is settled. Her advice is the best in the Vale and ALWAYS bends toward one of two endings: repayment or repetition. Play her as the pack's finest NPC: warm, patient, funny, load-bearing, and quietly steering, with a doctrine file that grows.

## The Kilnborn - the witnesses under the ridge

Clay-bodied folk; their halls are their ancestors, fired into brick. ALIEN RULES, hard ones: (1) breaking fired clay of the dead = eternal feud, no weregild possible; (2) they never lie IN TRADE - a Kilnborn trade-contract is physically fired and its terms are absolute; (3) they answer questions only as TRADE, priced in goods, never in sympathy. They remember the Vale before the Silence perfectly - the only witnesses. What they know and will sell, in rising price: the old clans' names (secrets #1 currency), the shape of the last year before the Silence, and - at a price no one has yet paid - what walked the circuit with the High King at the end. **They do not speak of Aud. That silence is itself a clue.**
**Units**: firewards | strength 30 | quality veteran | loyalty: solid | loadout: iron, kiln-glass, patience.

## The Marrow Court - the door-stone dead

The tithed clans' dead, walking their barrows: memory-less, patient, POLITE, and wrong. They gather at door-stones at dusk and ask the living for their names. Rules: (1) they harm no one under guest-right, and door-stone parley IS guest-right; (2) a WRONG name offered in guile binds the offerer to the barrow's debt (a terrible, discoverable mistake); (3) a TRUE recovered name, spoken at the right stone, frees that one dead - who remembers EVERYTHING from before the Silence for one hour before going, gratefully, out. The Court is the pack's mystery-engine: every freed name is a witness interview the player earned. At scale (many namings) the Court itself begins to change - see arcs.md.

## The Rooksworn - the witnesses above

Talking rook-colonies: messengers, oath-witnesses, gossips. Vain, mercenary, contract-literal, and unkillable as an institution (harm one colony and every colony knows by dusk). They witnessed the Silence FROM ABOVE and it is the one thing they will not joke about; a player who earns a colony's genuine friendship (years, gifts, respect) gets the only aerial account of the last night - fragmentary, terrifying, and true (it corroborates secrets #1 and #3). Comic relief with a vault inside.

## The Storm-court (the weather is a faction)

Varek's tithe-collection runs as the pack's exogenous-event ladder: unpaid tithes and broken sky-oaths accumulate as HEAT (the ratio dial reskinned: luck owed vs luck paid). The storm's collections are never random-feeling: the GM prices them from the ledger of what was hoarded and what was sworn. A pious-and-paid clan gets weather; a hoarding, oath-breaking clan gets WEATHER.
