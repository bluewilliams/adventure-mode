# The gods, the myths, and the Deepwalks (GM reference)

The Vale's religion is DEBT MADE WEATHER. Gods are not spell-lists; they are the physics of luck, priced. This file is the pack's economy-of-the-sacred: what worship buys, what quests pay, and the five authored god-stories whose KNOWLEDGE is the walkthrough. Myth-knowledge is inventory: recovered from Kilnborn trade, freed Marrow dead, rook gossip, rune-posts, and Aud (who edits what she shares). The player never reads this file; they EARN it in pieces, and the pieces are findable.

## The pantheon

- **Varek Stormfather** - sky, war, oaths. The Tithe-taker. Blessings: battle-luck, true winds, oath-weight. His law: what is sworn under sky is his to enforce; what is hoarded is his to collect.
- **Erda the Deep-Rooted** - earth, harvest, patience. Blessings: yield, healthy calving, ground-truth (her priests feel lies told standing on soil). Her law, the Vale's oldest: THERE IS ALWAYS ANOTHER WAY - no debt exists that can only be paid one way. (This line is load-bearing: it is the pack's escape hatch from secret #2, and Thyra's pregen hook.)
- **Halla of the Hundred Doors** - hearth, hospitality, the welcoming of the dead. Blessings: guest-luck, safe childbed, doors that hold. Her law: guest-right absolute; her curse (doors closing) is social death.
- **Skorn the Counter** - law, memory, weregild. Keeper of the Ledger of Names, in which every born name is written ONCE. Blessings: unbreakable witness, fair-price, the finding of lost things. The Silence is, in Skorn's terms, a page torn out - his priests feel its absence like a missing tooth.
- **Ysla of the Mists** - rivers, secrets, the drowned. Blessings: fish-luck, mist-cover, secrets kept. Price: she keeps yours too, and owning her silence cuts both ways.
- **Brann Twice-Burned** - forge, fire, the grudge. Blessings: iron-luck, warmth in the Long Dark, revenge that lands. The Kilnborn fire their dead in his rite; he is the bridge to them.
- **The Unnamed** - the trickster whose name was the FIRST tithe ever paid (the oldest story in the Vale). Worshippers legally do not exist: they cannot swear, inherit, owe weregild, or be ransomed - a status with horrifying uses and costs. Every moot keeps one empty chair for the Unnamed; who sits in it, and when, is a whole politics.
- **The Quiet God** - what the Vale calls the presence the Silence left behind. Not worshipped; PAID. No temple, no priest, no stories - which is itself the clue (secrets.md #1: the Quiet God is not a god at all).

## Worship as management

Each council seat carries a god (see npcs.md); shrines upgrade to temples with upkeep; and the ledger settles EVERY SEASON (settle_ops), while the **Reckoning rides the year's LAST settlement**: after the Long Dark's books close, the clan decides its tithe - what wealth becomes sacrifice, feast, and release, converting into next year's blessings - or hoards, and lets the Storm-court's heat rise. Blessings are recorded as clan assets with teeth (a "true winds" year measurably moves travel and raid clocks). Representing more gods on the council widens options; every seat empty is a category of luck the clan runs uninsured - the vacant forge-seat is the seeded example.

## Deepwalks (heroquests)

A Deepwalk re-enacts a god-story AT ITS TRUE SITE in the Vale, walking the story's stations in the flesh. The walker's competence matters (dice, honestly banded), but KNOWING THE STORY is the walkthrough: each station has a right act that follows the myth, and variant answers gated by who the walker is. Success pays blessings, treasures, and permanent growth; failure wounds, kills, or TITHES the walker (something is taken). Support helps (worshippers at the site, the season, prior stations honored). The five authored walks:

### 1. Varek and the First Wind (site: the king-stone saddle)
The story: Varek, young, stole the First Wind in a bag and would not share it; the world stilled and began to die; he paid it out again as the price of his own crown, keeping only the gust that carries oaths.
Stations: the Theft (take something that is genuinely yours to lose) -> the Stillness (endure the site's dead calm without speaking - hard for chiefs) -> the Paying-Out (release something hoarded, real, priced by the GM from the clan ledger) -> the Crowning Gust (swear one oath; it acquires Varek's weight forever).
Pays: oath-weight (the clan's sworn word gains mechanical force), a battle-luck season. Fails ugly if the walker hoards at station three: the storm collects the difference.

### 2. Erda's Third Answer (site: the drowned croft on the Graywater)
The story: the Hunger-King demanded Erda's harvest or her children; she gave neither and both; asked how, she said "there is always another way," and the way was that she paid HERSELF - a season of her own strength - and the Vale went hungry-but-alive while she slept.
Stations: the Demand (hear an impossible either-or without accepting its terms) -> the Search (find the third way in the station's riddle-scene; Kilnborn or freed-dead knowledge reveals the historical answer) -> the Self-Price (pay it from the walker's OWN sheet: a season of a stat, a bond strained, a scar).
Pays: the Third Answer blessing - once per year the clan may answer an either-or clock with a GM-priced third way. THIS is the lawful escape from the crown-debt (secrets #2), and Aud knows it, and it terrifies her hope into being.

### 3. Halla Opens the Hundred Doors (site: the barrow-field door-stones - the player's own land)
The story: when the first dead were homeless, Halla built them a hall with a hundred doors so any could find theirs; the living owe every door a welcome.
Stations: the Knock (parley at dusk with the Court, alone) -> the Naming (bring a TRUE recovered name and spend it here) -> the Threshold (invite one dead across as a GUEST for one night, under full guest-right, in the walker's own hall).
Pays: door-luck (the Marrow Court becomes navigable: the clan may parley safely at any barrow), and the guested dead's whole testimony. The single best mystery-engine unlock in the pack; also the scariest night of hospitality imaginable, played straight.

### 4. Skorn Counts the Torn Page (site: the moot-stone circuit, walked in one Long Dark night)
The story: when the page was torn from the Ledger (the Silence), Skorn did not rage; he began counting what remained, because a complete count of what is left DESCRIBES the hole.
Stations: the Count (name aloud, at each moot-stone, every true thing the clan has learned about the Silence - the quest literally audits the player's investigation) -> the Hole (at the king-stone, state what the count implies and be judged on honesty, not correctness) -> the Margin (write one recovered name back into the Ledger: spend it forever).
Pays: scaled to the truth of the count - at high truth, Skorn's Margin returns a FACT the clan could not otherwise get (GM picks the most load-bearing missing piece of secrets #1-3). The pack's designed mid-saga revelation valve.

### 5. The Unnamed Buys Back Nothing (site: wherever the walker is standing when they truly commit - the only walk with no fixed site)
The story: the Unnamed alone ever got a tithe REFUNDED - by proving the gods had taken more than was owed, arguing the case as nobody, since a nobody cannot be bribed, flattered, or threatened.
Stations: the Setting-Down (surrender name, torc, and titles for the walk's duration - the chief legally ceases to exist; the council rules; succession law goes live and STAYS live if the walker dies) -> the Audit (build the case that the Silence over-collected, from evidence the campaign actually gathered) -> the Pleading (argue it, at the king-stone, to the Quiet God's presence, as no one).
Pays: the endgame door. A won Pleading is one of the pack's three lawful Finale paths (see arcs.md). A lost one is the walker tithed entirely - name included - alive, unremembered, and still at the table (the boldest failure state in the pack; play it with total commitment).

## Treasures (samples; more emerge from play)

- **The Ninth Chair**: a moot-chair fired by the Kilnborn from pre-Silence clay; whoever sits it at a moot may ask one question the sitter must answer truly - ONCE per moot, and the chair remembers being lied to.
- **Aud's First Rune-Stick** (she is missing it; see secrets #3): the draft of the bargain, in her hand. Proof, and leverage on the one person leverage should be impossible against.
- **The Weather-Debt Tally**: a Kilnborn-fired tally of the High King's final accounting. Reads as gibberish until secrets #2 is half-known; then reads as a NUMBER, and the number is not fully paid.
- **A recovered true name**: the pack's signature treasure. Each is one line of inventory, one freed witness, one Ledger margin, one door-stone key. Never mass-produced; each one is a story.
