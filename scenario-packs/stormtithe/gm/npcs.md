# NPCs (GM sheets - seeds, grown by play)

Files under `NPCs/` at seeding for everyone the opening touches; the rest instantiate on first contact. Everyone named: drives, a voice anchor, a price OR a limit. The council are companions with full files - they are the advisor ring, and counsel (Running at Scale) runs through their sheets: what each would know, colored by god, temper, and grudge. Flaw hooks are springs the engine grows into traits and doctrine.

## The council (seed into the clan at creation)

Five seats by custom, one per god the clan keeps, plus the Unnamed's empty chair. The player's clan arrives with four filled and one argument:

- **Ulfar Skornsman** - law-speaker (Skorn). Memorized the law walking behind his blind father's plow; recites weregild schedules the way others hum. drives: the clan judged lawful by everyone, including enemies; a student worth teaching. Voice: quotes the schedule, then his opinion, in that order. Flaw hook: he is COMPULSIVELY honest in counsel even when honesty is tactically ruinous - and he is usually right, which makes it worse.
- **Ranna Hearthkeeper** - hearth-seat (Halla). Ran the clan's survival on the road; guest-right is her scripture because it saved them twice. drives: doors that hold; every child through their first winter. Voice: hospitality grammar, iron underneath ("you will eat first, and then you will explain yourself"). Flaw hook: she has already, quietly, been leaving food at the barrow-field's door-stones at dusk. It is either the wisest thing anyone in the Vale is doing or the beginning of an obligation nobody measured. Both.
- **Geir Wide-Grave** - war-thane (Varek). Dug too many graves on the road and counts spears the way Ulfar counts law. drives: never dig a grave he was too slow to prevent; test the Vosk line ONCE, properly, so the clan knows where it stands. Voice: force-arithmetic out loud ("they ride six good horses; we own two and a mule with opinions"). Flaw hook: his loyalty line is `solid`, but his patience with the chief's caution is a live counter the GM tracks honestly - he will never mutiny; he WILL start solving problems on patrol before they are assigned.
- **Sæunn Erda's-Ear** - earth-seat (Erda). Young for the seat; hears the ground the way her drowned mother did. drives: learn what the soil is lying about (her phrase; she is sensing the escrow); a harvest nobody prays through gritted teeth for. Voice: talks to the dirt mid-sentence, unembarrassed. Flaw hook: her ground-sense is REAL but unquantified - the pack's built-in oracle, honest and vague, and every clan in the Vale will eventually want to borrow, buy, or marry her.
- **The vacant forge-seat (Brann's)** - Torvald the forge-thane died in the passes, and with him the clan's iron-luck and its bridge to the Kilnborn rites. His tools ride in a locked chest nobody has reopened; his apprentice-daughter, **Inga Torvaldsdottir**, seventeen and better than anyone admits, has started firing small work at night without asking. Seating her is early, seating an outsider is politics, leaving it vacant is a category of luck uninsured through a Long Dark. The seat is a QUIET clock the pack never announces.
- **The empty chair (the Unnamed's)** - the argument. Ranna wants it left empty per custom; Geir wants it filled by the sharpest tongue available "because the gods gave us the chair for a reason." Whoever the player eventually seats there (a trickster foundling, a rook, nobody) is a saga-length decision the Vale watches.

## The household and the hooks

- **Bera** - the player's fostered niece, twelve, fearless, already trading rock-collection treasures with Rooksworn fledglings, which means she is accidentally building the clan's best intelligence network. Flaw hook: the rooks like her ENOUGH TO GOSSIP TO HER, and rook gossip is true, unvetted, and occasionally about her own clan's council.
- **Old Hakon** - the herd-master who carried the clan's calves through the passes on his own back. Knows every animal's name; refuses to name the new bull until "the land says it's staying." Flaw hook: he was born in the Vale's foothills - closer to the Silence than anyone admits - and his childhood lullabies are, unrecognized, pre-Silence songs the Marrow dead respond to.

## The Vale

- **Blind Aud** - law-speaker of the circuit (factions.md; secrets #3). Warm, patient, funny; the best counsel in the Vale, always slightly steering. Limit: she will never, under any pressure, speak of her own past - watch players notice the shape of that hole. Doctrine grows; the surety bias is standing from Day 0.
- **Ragnvald Vosk** - the raider-chief. Tests everyone once; word kept to the LETTER. Price: genuine respect is purchasable only with nerve. Flaw hook: privately proud that Eyvor argues with him; publicly cannot afford to be.
- **Eyvor Vosk** - the heir who counts. Sees the arithmetic her father's creed ignores. Approaches the player QUIETLY if the player looks like someone who also counts. The pack's best long-game alliance, and its most dangerous, since cultivating her looks exactly like plotting against Ragnvald.
- **Unn Ittra** - the matriarch. Gifts first, contracts second, always both. Limit: her family OUT of the Kilnborn obligation's direct line - she will trade almost anything for progress on that, including truths she should keep.
- **Sigrun Chief-Priestess** - Dreng's certainty. Beneath the sermons, a woman starving for one REAL sign. If the player's barrow-work produces genuine wonders, her arc forks: ally-in-awe or rival-in-denial, decided by how the player handles her hunger, not her doctrine.
- **Gest** - Dreng's drunk law-speaker who read the rune-post right. The Vale's saddest treasure: the truth of "hoddgild" (secrets #2) is in him, pickled. Sobering him up is a project with relapses, and every relapse follows someone TELLING him he was right.
- **Brekka Mool** - the chief who does arithmetic all night. Proud, exhausted, incorruptible by charity, winnable by RESPECT. The pack's moral thermometer: how the player treats Mool is how the Vale learns what the player is.
- **Hallgerd Sarn** - the dying trustee. Chooses, before the end, ONE person outside her blood to recite the founding trust to - chosen for character shown, not power held. The pack's most consequential deathbed, and the player is auditioning for it from Day 1 whether they know it or not.
- **Stig Sarn** - the heir who thinks the trust is superstition. Not wicked: MODERN. Every step of his (quiet inquiries, a discreet Ittra loan against "future considerations") is reasonable, and the sum is catastrophe. Give him the tragedy he is: a man selling a bomb because his elders forgot how to tell him it was one.
- **Ambassador Kesselclay** - the Kilnborn trade-voice: fired-clay politeness, absolute contracts, and the Vale's whole pre-Silence memory behind a price-list. Limit: never speaks of Aud; the refusal is itself, eventually, legible.
- **The Rook-Regent of Graywater Colony** - vain, brilliant, susceptible to well-crafted flattery and shiny tribute, contractually ruthless. Bera's friend. Keeper, ultimately, of the aerial account of the last night - shared only with a colony-friend of years' standing.
- **The Woman at the Door-Stone** - the first Marrow dead the player meets (starting-state). Patient, courteous, and carrying, unknown to herself, a name one vowel from the player's grandmother's. The pack's thesis statement in one NPC: the mystery is FAMILY.

## Voice anchors (one line each, read before they speak)

Ulfar: schedule first, opinion second. Ranna: feed you, then flay you. Geir: force-arithmetic aloud. Sæunn: addresses the dirt mid-sentence. Bera: reports rumors as treasure. Hakon: names are earned, even bulls'. Aud: proverbs that land two scenes later. Ragnvald: short, amused, letter-exact. Eyvor: numbers where feelings go. Unn: every gift has a clause. Sigrun: liturgy until surprised, then human. Gest: brilliant in the third drink's window. Brekka: apologizes for nothing, thanks precisely. Hallgerd: speaks like someone rationing words. Stig: reasonable, reasonable, reasonable. Kesselclay: contract grammar, kiln-warm courtesy. Rook-Regent: royal we, magpie soul. The door-stone dead: politeness a century out of date, and the question, always, gently: "do you know my name?"
