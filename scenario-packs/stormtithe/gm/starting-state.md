## The three problems that want the same week

1. **The door-stone** (tonight): the first plow-pass turned up a worked stone at the barrow-field's edge - a DOOR-STONE, meaning the "barrow-field" is not a field with barrows; it is a hall-yard, and the claim is inside something's threshold. At dusk a dead woman stands at it: courteous, century-fashioned, patient. She asks, gently, if anyone knows her name. Then, because guest-right holds at door-stones, she offers TRADE: she cannot remember her name, but she remembers HER WORK - she was somebody's memory-keeper - and she will tell the clan what their barrow-field truly holds, in exchange for the promise of a name, someday, honestly sought. (GM: her name is one vowel from the player's grandmother's - secrets #1's thesis, planted Day 0, payable YEARS later. The promise ledger takes it verbatim.) Ranna has already fed the stones; the Court noticed the courtesy.
2. **The welcome** (tomorrow): Ragnvald Vosk rides in with six - not a raid, a MEASUREMENT: eyes counting spears, gifts slightly too rich to refuse comfortably, and one engineered insult, mild and deniable, aimed at whoever looks most likely to flinch (Geir will not flinch; Geir is the problem). Whatever the player does becomes the Vale's first data-point on the new clan, and the pack's honest physics apply: nerve is respected, groveling is priced, and cleverness that dissolves the test entirely is the best outcome Ragnvald secretly hopes for.
3. **The guest** (tonight, overlapping the door-stone): Blind Aud arrives at dark, unannounced, asking hearth-room for the night - guest-right, cheerfully invoked. She is warm, funny, and helpful; she admires the torc a half-second too long; and over the fire she offers exactly one piece of unasked advice: "Plow AROUND the stones this year. Next year, ask them." (GM: honest advice, gently steering - she has waited a century; she needs this clan alive and slow. Sæunn, that night, tells the player the soil under the longhouse is lying about something. Both are right.)

## Opening scene

Cold open in the furrow: the plow jarring against the door-stone, oxen restive, Sæunn going still with both palms flat on the turned earth, Bera already climbing the stone to look for treasure, and Geir's voice from the bounds: riders on the drove road, six, unhurried, coming this way by tomorrow. The first night on their own land, and the land was somebody's first.
