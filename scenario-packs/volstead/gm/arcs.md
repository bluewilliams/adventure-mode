# Arc skeletons (hooks, escalations, multiple endings - never scripts)

The Question's campaign clock is Repeal (secrets.md #3): years away, visible early to careful readers, arriving regardless. Every arc below FEEDS the Question - none of them answers it. Player ambitions get promoted into arcs exactly like these.

## Arc 1: The 9th Ward is not free (opening pressure)

**Hook**: the starting situation - Sorrentino's tax man calls Saturday. Nunzio Fava himself, polite the first time.
**Escalations**: tribute demanded -> a corner business "changes its mind" about paying the player -> Bricks-vs-collector violence with witnesses -> Carmine hears his own name laughed at.
**Endings (any)**: pay and be owned (survivable, remembered, revisitable); negotiate a boundary through Fava (plants the Fava relationship); fight and win the ward (blood, heat, a REPUTATION - and Carmine's undivided attention); fight and lose (the outfit survives smaller, or the succession rule fires early); feed Carmine's overreach clock until the city does the fighting (the patient masterpiece).

## Arc 2: The route (the empire's spine)

**Hook**: Vico's maps, or Georges Fontaine's one-case courtesy, or a hijacked truck bleeding whiskey across Canal Street with nobody alive to claim it.
**Escalations**: first run (weather, hijack tithe, Bureau photographs) -> volume needs boats, a boathouse, a wire man -> the Carricks notice water traffic -> winter: the ice road, where trucks cross at night and sometimes don't.
**Endings**: a tithe deal with the Carricks (alliance-with-terms, Relationships.md); a war for the water (the Carricks' home game); the PARTNERSHIP play - Fontaine respect earned until the player IS the route and rivals buy from THEM; or the quiet read - Fontaine hedging into malt contracts teaches Repeal early, and the route becomes the thing the player sells at the top.

## Arc 3: The election of 1927 (the city decides itself)

**Hook**: Alderman Kessler's man asks the player's corner to "get out the vote." Everyone is buying precincts by spring.
**Escalations**: the machine leans; the reformer (backed quietly by Maxine's reporting) surges; the Temperance League marches; Brauer funds BOTH; Carmine's spectacle becomes a campaign issue; somebody offers the player real money to burn a rival's soup kitchen the week before the vote.
**Endings**: machine holds (business as usual, debts remembered both ways); reformer wins (raid schedules die, the dope license goes to market, chaos and opportunity); the player's OWN slate - aldermen first, the long game toward City Hall itself (the mayor ending is real and takes years); or the player publishes/leaks the dope truth and the election becomes a trial of the machine with the player holding the match.

## Arc 4: The ledger (the arithmetic men)

**Hook**: a subpoena nobody expected - not for the player (yet): for a front's grocer, whose books touch the player's books at exactly one seam.
**Escalations**: the tax unit works SIDEWAYS through fronts and suppliers; Aldo's 3% becomes evidence-shaped; Cole's ratio heat converges; the choice between a better accountant and a silenced one arrives wearing both faces.
**Endings**: books survive audit (the appearing-legal mask HOLDS - rare, earned, priceless); the sacrifice play (a front, a specialist, or a lieutenant goes down and STAYS BOUGHT in prison, or doesn't); flip risk (the subpoenaed grocer offered protection by Cole - the player's response defines them); or the Capone ending, avoided only by having read this arc as the pack's loudest warning.

## Arc 5: The Widow's proof (the city's buried bomb)

**Hook**: slow - Mae's clock fills in the background until a middle-depth clue (secrets.md #2) crosses the player's path by accident: pension-stamped bills in a payment, the harbor cop drinking near truth.
**Escalations**: Brendan notices anyone near the thread; the priest-watcher surfaces; Mae's proof reaches 6/8 and the city smells war coming without knowing why.
**Endings**: the war nobody deserves (proof completes as built - Carrick vs Brauer, the city burns, every outfit chooses or bleeds); the truth handed to Mae WHOLE (by the player, with evidence - grief, justice inside the family, and a debt from Mae worth more than territory); the truth sold to Brauer (peace bought, the player owned by what they know); or the truth held silently - the loaded gun the promise ledger tracks until the campaign's finale sweeps it.

## Standing arc rules

- **Succession is an arc, not a reset**: if the boss falls, the successor inherits every live arc at its current temperature, plus a funeral everyone attends with agendas.
- **Alliances have terms** (Running at Scale): every deal above lands in Relationships.md with its terms; every breach is an event with a date and witnesses.
- **The city escalates honestly**: heat, papers, and clocks - never GM mood. A quiet, disciplined player gets the quiet, disciplined game and wins it slower and cleaner.
- **The finale belongs to the Question**: whatever Repeal finds standing - warlord, magnate, mayor, prisoner, name on a Pigeon Hill headstone kept clean by people who remember - the closure sweep repays every thread above.
