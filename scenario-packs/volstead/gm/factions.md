# Factions (GM truth)

Every faction below is tracked at the outfit grain (Running at Scale): instantiate each as a GM file with its units, ledger, and clock at game start. Numbers here are Day 0 TRUTH; the player only ever holds estimates.

## The Brauer syndicate - the boardroom

**Agenda**: convert. Gustav Brauer knows Repeal is coming and intends to arrive there as a legitimate industrialist: legal brewery licenses (already quietly bought in Ontario), politicians banked, rivals bled or absorbed, books clean enough to survive an audit. Violence is a cost line, used precisely and deniably.
**Clock**: "Brauer goes respectable [0/12]" - each segment is a laundered asset, a bought office, a divested racket. When it fills, Brauer is UNTOUCHABLE by street means; only paper or scandal can reach him.
**Units** (in `GM/Brauer syndicate.md`):
- boardroom men | leader: Adele Voss (counsel) | strength 8 | quality veteran | loyalty: solid | doing: lawyers, accountants, fixers
- plant security | strength 30 | quality seasoned | loyalty: solid | doing: breweries, warehouses, Brickyard Row | loadout: shotguns, browning pistols | upkeep: 900/wk
- the quiet crew | leader: Emil Radu | strength 5 | quality veteran | loyalty: to Voss first | doing: the precise and deniable | loadout: knives, pistols, one Thompson nobody has seen
**Ledger truth**: ~$11,000/wk net; two-thirds already flows through LEGAL fronts. Brauer's weakness is exposure, not muscle: one honest audit of 1923's books ruins him, and Adele Voss knows where those books are.
**Disposition to a new boss**: a line item. Ignores you below a threshold; above it, offers to BUY you before bothering to break you. Brauer's offers are real, fair, and end with you owned.

## The Carrick Gang - the harbor

**Agenda**: hold. The docks, the local, the harbor police, Pigeon Hill's loyalty - and vengeance on Brauer for Declan Carrick, when Mae finally has proof worth a war. She has been collecting for three years.
**Clock**: "The Widow's proof [0/8]" - segments are witnesses, documents, deathbed talk. It is filling toward the WRONG answer (see secrets.md): the proof will point at Brauer because it was built to.
**Units**:
- dock crews | leader: Brendan Carrick (Mae's brother-in-law) | strength 45 | quality seasoned | loyalty: to the family | doing: the harbor, the local, cargo that walks | upkeep: 1100/wk
- the parish boys | strength 20 | quality green | loyalty: to Pigeon Hill more than Mae | doing: corners, collections, eyes
- Mae's own | leader: Fintan Shore | strength 6 | quality veteran | loyalty: solid | doing: what Mae says, only what Mae says | loadout: pistols, two Thompsons
**Ledger truth**: ~$6,500/wk; heavy costs (half the harbor precinct is on it). Cash-poor relative to size - the Carricks pay in jobs, favors, and belonging, which is why their loyalty runs deeper and their guns run older.
**Disposition**: territorial and fast. A new boss touching the water or the unions gets one warning delivered politely by two large men. The second visit is not polite. But the Carricks RESPECT nerve, keep their word to the letter, and make real allies - Mae's word, once given, has never broken. Getting it means giving something real about Declan.

## The Sorrentino outfit - the appetite

**Agenda**: grow. Corners, dope, girls, gambling - volume over discipline. Carmine buys men fast, burns them fast, and answers every insult with fire because he believes (correctly, for now) that fear is cheaper than payroll.
**Clock**: "Carmine overreaches [0/6]" - segments tick on each act of spectacle (a body in daylight, a torched business with a family upstairs). When it fills, the CITY moves: Cole's Bureau, the papers, and City Hall converge on him - and every neighbor gang picks over the carcass. A patient player can FEED this clock.
**Units**:
- tax collectors | leader: Nunzio Fava | strength 12 | quality seasoned | loyalty: paid, not loved | doing: the Flats collections | loadout: pistols, bats | upkeep: 700/wk
- the dope table | leader: "Doc" Bruno Gatti | strength 8 | quality seasoned | loyalty: to the money | doing: cutting plant above the Aurora club, pharmacy network
- muscle | strength 25 | quality green | loyalty: paid, not loved | doing: whatever Carmine shouted last | upkeep: 1000/wk
**Ledger truth**: ~$9,000/wk gross and leaking - the bulk is gambling, vice, and Flats protection; the dope network is a $1,500 sideline that happens to be the only untouchable thing Carmine runs (he thinks that's respect; it's a license - secrets.md #1). Skim at every level because loyalty was never bought, only rented. Nunzio Fava skims the most and is the most ambitious; he is also the most buyable senior man in the city, at the right moment, for the right future.
**Disposition**: the player's first collision (starting-state). Carmine hears "new boss in the 9th Ward" as either tribute or insult - nothing in between.

## City Hall - the fourth gang

Mayor Ambrose Quill's machine: aldermen priced by ward, contracts as currency, Chief of Police Harlan Voss (Adele's uncle - the families braid together) golfing with everyone. The machine's agenda is the machine: stay in. Election clock: "November 1927 [0/16]", ticking monthly; reform candidate, Temperance League, and every outfit's money all pull at it. THE MACHINE SECRETLY OWNS THE DOPE TRADE (secrets.md) - Sorrentino operates it on license. This is the pack's recontextualizing truth; guard it at three depths.

## The Bureau squad - the eleven honest feet

Agent **Everett Cole** plus five: Cole is INCORRUPTIBLE - no price, no lever, no exception; his squad is human (one drinks, one owes, one talks to a reporter he's sweet on). Agenda: the ROUTE, not the corner boys - Cole builds conspiracies upward and photographs everything. Clock: "Cole's case [0/10]" against whichever outfit currently shows him the most ratio-heat. The tax men behind him are slower and worse: they never raid, they SUBPOENA.

## Northside - the neighbors

Policy banks, clubs, credit unions under **Elias Crane**, who has run Northside's money for twenty years with fewer bodies than any downtown outfit and better books than Brauer. Deals honestly, remembers everything, extends exactly one courtesy to a new boss. His counsel is the best in the city and he gives it only to people who bring him something true. Northside is NOT for taking: it is for dealing with, or leaving alone - the pack treats it as a sovereign neighbor with its own interior life, not a territory tile.

## The supply - the Fontaine house

Port Sarnelle, Ontario: the Fontaine brothers' export house sells legally by Canadian law, to anyone, cash. The ROUTE is the contested thing - boats, ice roads in winter, hijackers (the Carricks take a tithe of everything on the water they don't like). The Fontaines keep a ledger of every buyer. Cole would trade a year of his life for it. So would Brauer.
