# NPCs (GM sheets - seeds, grown by play)

Files under `NPCs/` at seeding for anyone the opening touches; the rest instantiate on first contact. Every named person: drives, a voice anchor, a price OR a limit. Crew candidates carry FLAW HOOKS - the engine grows their traits and doctrine from play; the pack only loads the springs.

## The player's starting people (seed into the outfit)

- **Stefan "Steppy" Kowal** - the loyal one. Drove for the player before there was anything to drive. drives: prove the player chose right; a house on Pigeon Hill someday. Voice: short sentences, "boss" at the end of them. Flaw hook: he is the one Sorrentino's man offered double this morning - and he hasn't decided how to tell you, which is not the same as deciding to take it.
- **Rose Antonelli** - the sharp one. Kept book for a Flats grocer's association until it got absorbed; counts cards, counts people. drives: never be poor again; run something with her name on it. Voice: answers questions with better questions. Flaw hook: she is ALREADY running a small card game on your corner without asking - your first management problem is your best employee's side hustle.
- *(third, if the pregen brings one)* **the cousins / the six / the debt-holder** per pregen choice - instantiate with the same care.

## Crew candidates (met through play; jagged on purpose)

- **Janusz "Bricks" Adamczyk** - fists 5, brains 2, heart 6. Beloved in the Flats, incapable of subtlety, cries at weddings. The beating he delivers is honest and the witnesses like him too much to talk. Flaw hook: cannot pass the Carrick corner bar without answering an insult; he WILL restart that war twice a season unless managed.
- **"Clean" Willie Novak** - a knife man of terrifying quiet competence. Flaw hook: sent to intimidate, he escalates - the stat mismatch rule made flesh. Refuses to work Sundays. Nobody knows where he lives.
- **Pearl Okafor** - wire man's ear, switchboard operator at the Lakeview exchange. Hears half the city's sins nightly. drives: her brother's tuition; out of the Flats by thirty. Flaw hook: she sells the SAME information twice when squeezed - loyalty solid only while she feels protected.
- **Aldo Bruni** - the accountant the player can actually get (the good ones are taken). Competent, fast, and skims 3% as a professional courtesy he genuinely believes is standard. Flaw hook: catching him is a fork - fired, he knows everything; kept, the skim IS the leash, and he respects a boss who names it.
- **Father Muldane** - Pigeon Hill's priest. Not crew, never crew - but the neighborhood's true boss of hearts, keeper of one catastrophic confession (secrets.md), and the only man both Mae Carrick and the player's grandmother obey.
- **Officer Danny Wrenn** - patrol cop, buyable, friendly like a toll booth. Flaw hook: his sergeant is the honest one, and Danny's envelopes are funding a transfer request AWAY from her.
- **Sgt. Cora Alvey** - the honest sergeant. Cannot be bought; CAN be dealt with - she trades in quiet neighborhoods, not arrests. A protected block that stays genuinely peaceful earns her blind eye to the peace's price. Limit: bodies. One body on her blocks and every arrangement dies.
- **Maxine Aurand** - crusading reporter, Lakeside Ledger. drives: the machine, on the front page, in handcuffs. Flaw hook: she is RIGHT about the dope story and eighteen months from proving it; whoever feeds her middle-depth clues aims a citywide gun - and Cole's squad-man is sweet on her, which is a channel flowing both directions.
- **Georges Fontaine** (the younger brother) - the export house's charming face. Sells to anyone once; sells TWICE to people with manners and boats that leave on time. His respect is the route's real license; his ledger is the pack's bomb.
- **Elias Crane** - Northside's banker. Twenty years, fewer bodies than any downtown outfit, better books than Brauer. Extends one courtesy to a new boss and watches what they do with it. His counsel is the city's best and is bought only with something TRUE. Voice: never hurries, never repeats himself.
- **Nunzio Fava** - Sorrentino's tax captain (see factions, secrets). Ambitious, skimming, buyable for a FUTURE only. The most dangerous hire in the pack and the best, possibly both.
- **Adele Voss** - Brauer's counsel. The smartest person in the city and the least free; keeper of the conversion timetable and the machine's insurance book. Her limit is her nephew, a Bureau squad-man she will not see ruined.
- **Mae Carrick** - the Widow. Grief hardened into governance. Her word, once given, has never broken; her proof-clock is a bomb her own dock boss keeps feeding. What she does when the truth lands depends entirely on WHO hands it to her, HOW, and holding WHAT.
- **Agent Everett Cole** - eleven honest feet (his squad's five pairs, minus the drink, the debt, and the crush). Photographs everything. Fair to a fault; incorruptible without exception. The player's most dangerous constant and, played long and clean, something almost like an ally against worse.

## Voice anchors (one line each, read before they speak)

Steppy: short sentences, "boss." Rose: better questions back. Bricks: apologizes mid-swing. Willie: agrees, then silence. Pearl: whispers even in private. Aldo: percentages as adjectives. Muldane: absolution grammar, even ordering lunch. Alvey: verbs, no adjectives. Maxine: everything is a headline draft. Georges: French when lying, English when dealing. Crane: never hurries. Fava: compliments with hooks in them. Voss: contract clauses as small talk. Mae: present tense about Declan. Cole: writes while you talk, quotes you back later, verbatim.
