# Secrets (GM only - never surface undug)

Three load-bearing truths, each seeded at three depths. Discovery is a tracked state: surface signs are free, middle clues reward a deliberate dig, the truth is DEFENDED.

## 1. The machine owns the dope (the recontextualizing truth)

**Truth**: Mayor Quill's machine OWNS the morphine and heroin trade. Sorrentino operates it on license: the pharmacy network runs through licenses City Hall issues, raid schedules come down from the Chief's office, and a third of the take goes up in "campaign contributions" laundered through the Quill Civic Fund. Carmine is not the fourth power in the city; he is an employee who thinks he's a partner. The city's whole moral geometry inverts here: the loudest gang is the tamest dog, and the reform-vs-machine election is really a fight over who holds the dope license.

- **Surface** (free, anyone watching): Sorrentino's dope operations are never raided. Stills get padlocked, card rooms get tipped over - the pharmacies never.
- **Middle** (a deliberate dig): a pharmacist's license renewal signed personally by an alderman who votes dry; Doc Gatti's ledger page where a third of every week goes to "C.F." with no name; a Bureau raid on the Aurora club called off by phone, and the desk sergeant's log shows the call came from City Hall's exchange.
- **Deep** (defended): the Quill Civic Fund's second book, kept by Adele Voss's junior clerk as Brauer's INSURANCE against the machine. Getting it means beating Brauer's lawyers or turning the clerk - and holding it makes the player the most hunted person in Calder Bay. Publishing it breaks the machine; selling it buys almost anything; sitting on it is a loaded gun the ledger tracks.

## 2. Declan Carrick's real killer

**Truth**: Brauer did not kill Mae's husband. Declan was killed by **Brendan Carrick** - his own brother, now Mae's dock boss - because Declan was about to sell the union's pension book to Brauer to cover gambling debts. Brendan shot him on the Fontaine dock, framed the scene toward Brauer's quiet crew, and has spent three years quietly FEEDING Mae's proof-clock with manufactured evidence, because the war it will start solves his problem forever. The feud that structures the city is built on a lie told by the man who profits from both sides of it.

- **Surface**: Brendan never works the Fontaine dock and never lets anyone else load there at night; old-timers call it respect for Declan.
- **Middle**: the Fontaines' buyer ledger shows Declan's last purchase was paid with pension-stamped bills; a retired harbor cop who saw a man RUN toward Pigeon Hill that night, not toward Brickyard Row - he told no one because the man was family.
- **Deep** (defended): Brendan's confession exists - he told it drunk, once, to Father Muldane in confession. The priest will die before repeating it, but Brendan doesn't trust that, and the FEAR is discoverable: he has a man watching the priest. Anyone who follows THAT thread backward holds the city's most explosive truth. Mae learning it wrong (half of it) starts the war anyway - at the player, if they're holding the pieces.

## 3. Repeal has already started

**Truth**: the trade is dying on a schedule almost nobody reads. Brauer has bought Ontario brewery licenses, warehouse options, and two state legislators; his "goes respectable" clock IS the Repeal countdown made private. The Fontaine brothers know (their export volume is quietly hedging into legal malt contracts). The Question's campaign clock lives here: when Repeal arrives, booze margins die in a season, and every empire built only on whiskey is a museum. Players who read the signs early can convert; players who don't inherit Carmine's future.

- **Surface**: newspapers - wet politicians winning elsewhere, the Temperance League's rallies getting older and smaller.
- **Middle**: Fontaine contracts with legal malt buyers; Brauer divesting stills while everyone else builds them; Elias Crane, who read it first, moving Northside money into property and credit.
- **Deep**: Adele Voss's conversion timetable - the single most valuable strategic document in the pack. Brauer would trade blood for its secrecy; Crane would trade counsel for a look; Cole would see in it the conspiracy map of the whole lake.

## Standing GM notes

- **Cole is incorruptible, PERIOD** - no price, no hostage, no exception, ever; his squad leaks, he does not. He is also FAIR: he keeps his word, values truth from anyone including the player, and a boss who hands him a bigger fish gets exactly what was promised and not a dollar more. His file grows doctrine like anyone's.
- **Nunzio Fava** (Sorrentino's tax captain) is the buyable senior man - but only for a FUTURE, never for cash alone. Whoever offers him a chair builds a knife into their own house: his loyalty line reads `loyalty: to Nunzio` forever.
- **The Fontaine buyer ledger** is the pack's portable bomb: every truth above touches it. It stays in Port Sarnelle, in a safe, behind Canadian law and two brothers who like the player exactly as much as the player is useful.
- **Succession truth**: if the player dies, these secrets DON'T reset - the successor inherits the outfit's earned knowledge (the record), not the boss's private suspicions (context). What was never written died with them.
