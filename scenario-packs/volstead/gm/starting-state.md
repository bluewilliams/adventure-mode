## The three problems that want the same weekend

1. **The shipment** (tonight): forty cases of Fontaine rye - the player's whole bankroll - sit in the Kowal family boathouse on the canal, landed last night. SOMEBODY TALKED: by midnight, word reaches the player (Pearl's switchboard whisper, or a kid paid to run) that men asked the night watchman about the boathouse. Truth (GM): it was Rose's card game - a player bragged to a Carrick parish boy. The Carricks intend a tithe lesson, not a war: take ten cases, leave a note. If the player moves the load first, they arrive at an empty boathouse and start asking harder questions. Every choice teaches the engine: guarding is a unit order, moving is logistics with counts, negotiating is Relationships-with-terms, hiding it in the newsstand puts the whole bankroll behind their only front.
2. **The tax man** (Saturday noon): Nunzio Fava, polite, alone by choice, at the corner: "Mr. Sorrentino heard someone new was doing well in the 9th. He's happy for you. It's forty a week, and he never has to be happier." Arc 1 opens. Fava NOTICES things (the nearly-empty cigar box's meaning, Rose's competence, whether the player's coffee hand shakes) and reports what serves Nunzio.
3. **The offer** (already happened, this morning): a Sorrentino runner offered Steppy double to walk away and say what he saw. Steppy hasn't told the player yet - he's been rehearsing how since breakfast. If the player learns of it from anyone but Steppy (Pearl heard; Rose suspects), the telling becomes a loyalty scene either way. Steppy's own resolution, if left alone: he tells them Sunday, ashamed it took two days. First loyalty tag in play, first counsel scene, first proof the outfit layer breathes.

## Opening scene

Cold open at the corner, Friday close: Rose counting the day's take without being asked, Steppy too quiet by the door, the lake wind finding the gaps in the boards - and the kid from the canal arriving out of breath with the boathouse news. Three problems, one weekend, $280, and a city that hasn't heard the player's name yet. It will.
