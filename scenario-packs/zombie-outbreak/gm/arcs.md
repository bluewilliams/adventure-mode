# WHITEOUT - Arc Skeletons (GM)

Skeletons, not scripts. Each arc has a hook, escalation beats, and multiple endings; play fills in everything else. Arcs run on the timeline in `secrets.md` even if ignored.

## Arc 1: The Toll (Kittredge)

- **Hook**: the paint census reaches the player's block; a struck circle means a raid is queued on someone the player knows.
- **Escalation**: road tax -> door tax -> conscription offers -> the insulin payroll visibly failing -> Kittredge forced toward either atrocity or collapse.
- **Endings**: break the Tollmen (tanker/payroll/mutiny); inherit them (the civilian half follows a competent alternative); bargain a real truce (Kittredge keeps his word forever); or watch Iron Lake become a tollbooth with houses.

## Arc 2: The Flock (Voss)

- **Hook**: someone the player cares about, cold and hungry, walks up the hill toward the singing.
- **Escalation**: warmth and welcome -> confession -> small leverages -> the selection doctrine at capacity (week 10) -> quiet exiles becoming less quiet.
- **Endings**: expose him (ledger + Corrine's testimony); replace him (the Congregation's need for shelter is real and doesn't die with his myth); become his successor (dark path, fully allowed); or a lakeside grave and sixty people with nowhere to put their faith.

## Arc 3: The Voice (Ruth)

- **Hook**: 1610 AM reads a name the player knows. Or the player's own.
- **Escalation**: finding Birch Ridge -> the fuel countdown -> factions maneuvering for the notebook -> the night the radio goes silent (week 9 if unhelped).
- **Endings**: keep the lighthouse lit (fuel is a faction-scale problem, not a fetch quest); the notebook weaponized by whoever takes it; Ruth's sister surfacing in the horde data (only if the player connects Marsh's work to the name-lists); the region going dark and what that does to everyone's shortwave-thin hope.

## Arc 4: The Melt (the season itself)

- **Hook**: a warm week in late winter. A walker everyone knew was frozen solid isn't where it froze.
- **Escalation**: Marsh's research -> the horde math -> the water tower's strategic weight -> factions that spent all winter fighting each other realizing the real clock too late.
- **Endings**: an evacuation (to where?); a defense of Iron Lake that requires every faction and every grudge on the table; a controlled burn of the Highway 53 corridor (Marsh knows why this might work and what it costs); or spring.

## Companion seeds

Recruitable early, each with a personal arc that intersects a faction arc:

- **Sam Prentice**, 28, lineman, the player's starting neighbor (see starting-state): steady hands, night terrors, a wife on Ruth's read-list he hasn't found. Romance-capable. His arc crosses Arc 3.
- **Oskari Kettunen**, 15, if pried loose from the Tollmen's grooming: knows every fence-gap in town. His arc crosses Arcs 1 and Marta's trust.
- **Corrine Ash** (see NPCs): the pack's sharpest edge and its conscience. Romance-capable. Her arc IS Arc 2's fuse.
