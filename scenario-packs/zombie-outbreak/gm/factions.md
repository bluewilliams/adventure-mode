# WHITEOUT - Factions (GM)

Each faction has an agenda, resources, and a doctrine. They act on the timeline in `secrets.md` whether or not the player engages.

## The Tollmen

- **Who**: ~30 armed survivors built around the remnant of the Virginia, MN National Guard checkpoint; led by former staff sergeant **Dane Kittredge**. Uniform scraps, one working plow truck ("the Ram"), a fuel tanker they cannot fully defend, real fire discipline.
- **Agenda**: control movement. Roads are the only wealth that matters in winter; they tax travel, trade, and eventually doors (the paint census).
- **Doctrine**: predictable brutality. They keep their word, both promises and threats, because reputation IS their weapon. They are not cruel for fun; they are cruel on schedule.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: the fuel tanker is guarded by six people on a rotation with a gap at shift change (observation); Kittredge's authority rests on paying his people in insulin and antibiotics from a hijacked FEMA pallet, and the pallet is running out (capture/defector); half the Tollmen were checkpoint civilians who wanted safety, not banditry, and would break from Kittredge if shown a survivable alternative (social, long arc).

## The Congregation

- **Who**: 60+ survivors packed into Good Shepherd Lutheran on the hill; led by **Elias Voss**, a hospice chaplain who arrived with the convoys. Warmth, numbers, singing you can hear across the lake on still nights, and a perimeter of true believers.
- **Agenda**: publicly, sanctuary for all. Actually: Voss believes the infection is judgment and that he can read who is "marked." Admission requires confession; Voss's "discernment" decides who eats first, who watches the walls, and eventually who is quietly exiled to die.
- **Doctrine**: soft power. Food, heat, belonging, absolution. The Congregation does not raid; it absorbs the desperate and makes leaving feel like damnation.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: Voss keeps a ledger of every confession and reads it like a hand of cards; stealing it breaks his hold (infiltration); his first "discernment" exile, a teacher named Corrine Ash, is alive, frostbitten, and furious in the ice-fishing shacks on the north shore, and she can testify (exploration); Voss is diabetic and dependent on the same dwindling insulin the Tollmen control, a secret supply line neither side admits (cross-faction discovery, the pack's deepest lever).

## Kettunen's Yard

- **Who**: 9 survivors in the fenced compound of Kettunen Salvage & Diesel; matriarch **Marta Kettunen**, 61, third-generation scrapyard owner. Welded plate, dogs, wood heat, a machine shop, and the only people in town thinking about spring.
- **Agenda**: outlast everyone. Trade fair, trust slow, fortify, and quietly secure the water tower before anyone else realizes it matters.
- **Doctrine**: transactional decency. The Yard deals straight and shoots second, but nobody sleeps inside the wire until they've bled for the Yard somehow.
- **Weaknesses (discoverable)**: they are nine people defending two acres, catastrophically thin at night (observation); Marta's grandson Oskari sneaks out to trade with (and is being groomed as an informant by) the Tollmen (social); the Yard's well pump is electric and their generator is one part-failure from silence, which is why Marta wants the gravity-fed tower (technical).

## The Quiet House (rumor tier)

- **Who/What**: survivors whisper about a house north of Birch Ridge where the lights are always on and nobody who knocks comes back. GM truth: a retired wildlife biologist, **Dr. Lena Marsh**, running generator-heated greenhouse experiments on frozen infected tissue. She is 80% of the way to understanding the thaw problem and completely out of her depth on the human threat. She shoots trespassers because the last group she let in stole her seed stock.
- **Purpose**: the pack's knowledge endgame. Whoever connects Marsh's thaw research to the Highway 53 horde gets the spring countdown in hard numbers, and the moral weight of deciding who else learns it.
