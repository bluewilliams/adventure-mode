# WHITEOUT - GM Secrets

SPOILERS. Player-facing text must never contain unflipped facts from this file.

## The infection (the real rules)

- **Vector**: saliva and blood to broken skin. Bites are near-certain infection (95%). Scratches that draw blood: roll, ~1 in 4. Blood splatter to eyes/mouth: rare but possible. Frozen infected material is inert until warmed; this is not yet understood by anyone alive in Iron Lake.
- **Course**: fever within hours, death in 12-48 hours (faster with exhaustion or hypothermia), reanimation 10-90 minutes after death. Amputation within ~10 minutes of a bite on a limb has actually worked twice in this world. Nobody in Iron Lake knows a confirmed case; Doc Halloran suspects it.
- **Cold biology**: the dead slow drastically below about -10C: sluggish, near-dormant when frozen solid. A frozen-solid walker reanimates when thawed. Spring is a countdown the player should eventually feel in their gut. The horde that overran Duluth is currently frozen in and around the Highway 53 corridor. It will move when the melt comes.
- **Sound and heat**: they orient to sound first, then heat contrast against the cold. A chimney at night is a beacon. Iron Lake's survivors have learned the sound part; almost nobody has connected the heat part. That gap kills people.
- **No cure. No immunity.** Anyone who claims either is wrong or lying. Elias Voss claims both. He is lying.

## The world beyond the radio

- There is no functioning government relief inside 300 miles. The National Guard checkpoint at Virginia, MN collapsed in week 2; its survivors and materiel became the seed of the Tollmen (see factions).
- The voice on 1610 AM is Ruth Okafor, broadcasting alone from the KQIL transmitter shack on Birch Ridge, reading names of confirmed survivors and confirmed dead from a notebook. Her generator has about three weeks of fuel left at current use. She is the closest thing the region has to a lighthouse, and three factions have started to realize the strategic value of the name list.
- Iron Lake's water tower is intact and gravity-fed. Whoever controls water when spring comes controls the town. Only Marta Kettunen (see NPCs) has thought this far ahead.

## The paint markings

Fresh paint sigils on doors are the Tollmen's census: circle = paid tribute, struck circle = refused (raid queued), triangle = infected inside (avoid), X = cleared/looted. The player can decode this through observation, capture, or Marta.

- Week 9: Ruth's generator dies; the radio goes silent; regional survivor morale collapses
- Week 10: Voss's "sanctuary" at the Lutheran church reaches capacity and starts its selection doctrine (see arcs)
