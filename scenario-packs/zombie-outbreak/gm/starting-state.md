# WHITEOUT - Starting State (GM)

## Opening: Day 36, first light, minus eighteen

The player character has spent two weeks in the crawlspace-heated back rooms of **Lindqvist Hardware** on Main Street with one other survivor: **Sam Prentice** (companion seed, lineman, steady, sleepless). It has been quiet. Quiet is never safe.

Cold open with all three pressures landing inside the first scene:

1. **The stove is dying.** Last night burned the final split wood; the pallet scraps left will not get through tonight at minus twenty. Warmth is now a today problem.
2. **Fresh paint.** Sometime before dawn, someone put a **circle, struck through** on the hardware store's front door (Tollmen census: refused tribute, raid queued). Neither the player nor Sam has ever been asked for tribute. Someone marked them wrong, or marked them on purpose.
3. **The radio.** Sam's battery shortwave catches 1610 AM mid-list. Give the player three names, then: a name the player character knows. Choose it during character creation from "who do you have left to lose" and do not warn them.

First menu should offer (roughly): deal with the heat problem, investigate/remove the mark, chase the radio name, fortify and watch, or something else. Every option is real; every option costs the day's light.

## Character creation (interview, in-fiction framing)

Three questions, asked as memories surfacing in the cold dark before the stove dies:

1. **Who were you before?** (occupation seeds 2-3 starting traits and one tool of the trade)
2. **Why are you still alive when better people aren't?** (the answer seeds one hard-earned survival trait and one scar: a condition, a debt, or a reputation)
3. **Who or what do you have left to lose?** (seeds the radio name, a bond, and the character's private clock)

Aptitudes: assign the 4/3/3/2/2 spread from the answers (who they WERE places the 4; how they survived places the 3s). Do not show the numbers; reflect them back as fiction ("your hands know engines" beats "Finesse 4").

Starting inventory: winter clothes (good), one trade tool, one improvised or true weapon (player's call within fiction; ammo counted if firearm, single digits), half a day of food, matches, and one meaningful personal object (player defines; it will matter).

## Board state at day 36

- Tollmen: census-painting Iron Lake's core blocks; the struck circle on the store is queued for enforcement within ~2 days
- Congregation: at 60+, still welcoming, seven weeks from capacity doctrine
- Kettunen's Yard: trading fair through the fence, wary of everyone since a fence-probe last week (it was Tollmen scouts)
- Ruth: broadcasting nightly, ~21 days of generator fuel
- The lake ice: thick, safe, and the fastest way to move unseen (and the coldest)
- Weather: clear and brutal for three days, then a front the GM should use like a hammer
